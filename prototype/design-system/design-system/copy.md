# Copywriting — full canon (Solve Education!)

> Load for any **substantial** copy task: marketing, long-form, headlines, learner stories, per-audience.
> For quick **in-product UI strings** (buttons / errors / empty states), `design-system/brand-voice.md` is the fast path.
> Roof message everything ladders up to: **"Turning learning into livelihood. Powered by behavioural science."**

## COPY · Voice (who we sound like)
| Trait | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Human-Centered | Warm, real people & outcomes, not abstractions | "Amara stayed in school because she could see her progress." (not "retention improved 47%") |
| Behavioral-Science-Minded | Explain how/why, grounded in evidence; no overselling | "Consistent small wins build confidence. That's why we celebrate every milestone." |
| Bold | Say what needs saying | "Education is expanding. But opportunity is not." |
| Inclusive | Accessible, no gatekeeping; explain acronyms | Avoid jargon; reflect diverse learners |
| Credible | Trust via specificity & measured claims, never hype | Cite Impact Genome; real numbers |
| Optimistic | Grounded, earned optimism | "Persistence becomes possible when the system is designed for it." |

Emotional thread — every piece evokes ≥1: **Mobility · Opportunity · Dignity**.

## COPY · Core messaging
- **Roof (headlines/positioning):** "Turning learning into livelihood. Powered by behavioural science."
- **Core message (longer-form/about/value-prop):** "We design learning systems that keep young people engaged long enough to build measurable, income-relevant skills. Using our GAIN Framework—Gamification, AI Coach, Incentives, and Network—we turn persistence into progress, and progress into livelihood pathways."
- **Why we exist:** 600M+ students in school below minimum proficiency; many more out of school. In an AI-driven economy, education alone no longer guarantees earning power. Too many disengage before building market-relevant skills.

## COPY · Four pillars (use the content ratio across a campaign)
- **P1 · Human Transformation (40%)** — what changed for a learner & why it matters. Lead with the *before*; be specific about the change & when; tie to livelihood; first-person; quantify. *Avoid:* vague ("life-changing"), over-polished, struggle-free stories.
- **P2 · GAIN Explained Simply (30%)** — we design for consistency via GAIN. One sentence per component + the behavioral principle; analogies. *Avoid:* "proprietary", "engineered behavior", "guaranteed outcomes", oversimplified science.
- **P3 · Proof & Measurement (20%)** — what we measure, what improved, what it cost. Always cite source; comparable methodology; show cost-effectiveness; outcome + process metrics. *Avoid:* sweeping benchmarks, outcome inflation, correlation-as-causation, vague metrics.
- **P4 · Scale with Integrity (10%)** — built to last via Foundation (safeguards mission) + Holdings (scales delivery). Focus on trust/accountability, not scale hype. *Avoid:* "becoming for-profit", IP hype, over-complication.

**Key proof points (use when relevant, cite source):** 90%+ program retention · **$6 per successful outcome** (Impact Genome, independently verified) · 30× more cost-effective than global benchmark · program-specific (e.g. "Boeing Fly High: X% secured employment").

## COPY · Language guardrails
- **COPY-01 DO:** sentence case · active voice · secondary-school reading level (Flesch-Kincaid 8–10) · short sentences (avg 12–15 words) · contractions · specific concrete language ("600 million students") · real numbers · show-don't-tell (evidence over adjectives) · positive framing · address the learner directly.
- **COPY-02 DON'T:** corporate jargon (synergy/leverage/disruptive/ecosystem/scalable) · hyperbole (guaranteed/revolutionary/game-changing/proprietary) · ALL CAPS (except logos/acronyms) · condensed/decorative fonts · exaggeration ("transforms" for "improves") · academic jargon unexplained · assume privilege · unkeepable promises · ignore the emotional thread.

## COPY · Terminology
**Approved (use liberally):** "Turning learning into livelihood" · "Persistence becomes possible" · "Income-relevant skills" · "Real, market-relevant skills" · "Building blocks of livelihood" · "Measurable outcomes" · "Our GAIN Framework" (always "Our", capitalized) · "Behavioral science".

**Avoid → better:** Disruption→"redesigning how learning sticks" · Proprietary→"built from behavioral research" · EdTech platform→"learning system" · Users/UX→"Learners / learning experience" · Engagement metrics→"consistency / showing up" · Drop-off rate→"keep learners in the game longer" · At-risk youth→"young people outside opportunity" · Skill gap→"skills to build" · Leverage→"use / apply / build on" · Guaranteed outcomes→"designed for persistence".

## COPY · Tone by audience
- **Learners (primary):** encouraging, clear, celebratory, direct; "you"+"we"; celebrate small wins; avoid data-heavy language; honest about effort. *e.g.* "You've completed 5 modules this month. That's consistency. Keep going—the next level unlocks when you finish the quiz."
- **Parents/Guardians:** reassuring, evidence-based, practical; show measurable progress; address safety/time/cost; connect to livelihood.
- **Funders/Partners:** credible, strategic, impact-focused; lead with evidence (Impact Genome); cost-effectiveness + sustainability; theory of change; scale without hype.
- **Educators/Partners:** collaborative, evidence-informed; respect expertise; GAIN as support not replacement; what the AI Coach takes off their plate.
- **Social:** lead with emotion/insight; one proof point or story element; line breaks for readability; conversational.

## COPY · What NOT to say
**Absolute prohibitions:** "Guaranteed job placement" · "Revolutionary/Game-changing/Transformative" (show, don't claim) · "All students will succeed" · "We solve education" · "Proprietary algorithm" · dark patterns ("Spots filling up!", "Limited time!") · deficit language without context · false comparisons · jargon without explanation.

**Tone guardrails (say → not):** "designed for persistence" → "guarantee engagement" · "progress in three weeks" → "results guaranteed in 3 weeks" · "30× more cost-effective (Impact Genome)" → "30× better than competitors" · "real skills for real jobs" → "the only program that works" · "behavioral science shows…" → "science proves…".

## COPY · Length & density
- **H1:** ≤10 words; emotional thread; front-load benefit ("Persistence becomes possible.").
- **H2:** 10–15 words; explain/specify the headline.
- **Body:** ≤3 sentences/paragraph; avg 12–15 words/sentence; short paragraphs.
- **Learner stories:** 300–500w long / 100–150w short; before → turning point → concrete outcome; direct quotes.
- **Case studies/reports:** tables + visuals; ≤200w prose per section; lead with the proof point.

## COPY · Approval checklist (must pass)
- **Brand fit:** ladders to "Turning learning into livelihood"? avoids jargon/hype? human-centered & warm? shows evidence not superiority? learner/partner centered (not the org)?
- **Tone:** optimistic but grounded? evokes Mobility/Opportunity/Dignity? honest about guarantees? inclusive? credible via specificity?
- **Mechanics:** sentence case? active voice? sentences avg 12–15w? short paragraphs? FK 8–10? contractions? jargon explained?
- **Pillar:** fits one of four pillars? ratio balanced (40/30/20/10)? approved terminology? avoids prohibited phrases?
- **Learner safety:** no false promises? no dark patterns? respects agency (effort not just outcomes)? no deficit/stigmatizing language?
- **Final test:** read it aloud — does it sound like someone you'd trust to teach you something that matters? If no → revise.
