# Research Plan: Indonesian Teacher EdTech Onboarding & Activation Literature

- **Status:** Approved (Parallel Literature Study)
- **Type:** benchmark (Literature & Empirical Research Focus)
- **Goal it serves:** Ground our Android app onboarding and registration flow for Indonesian teachers using quantitative and empirical academic/industry findings on deferred registration, loss aversion, and Indonesian educator TAM.

## Key research questions
1. **Registration Timing & Conversion:** What empirical evidence exists regarding the exact drop-off rates of upfront registration ("wall-first") vs. deferred registration ("guest-first / try-first") in mobile apps and EdTech platforms?
2. **Endowment Effect & Loss Aversion Mechanics:** How does completing an initial task (e.g., Module 1 or generating an RPP/Lesson Plan) alter the psychological willingness of users to complete registration when framed as "saving progress / securing credentials"?
3. **Indonesian Teacher Adoption Drivers (TAM & PMM):** What specific factors predict sustained digital tool adoption among Indonesian teachers across urban and remote regions, and how do recognized PD credentials (*sertifikasi / jam pelatihan*) influence extrinsic vs. intrinsic motivation?

## Investigation & Literature Search Plan
### Pillar 1: Mobile Onboarding & Registration Drop-Off Studies
- **Sources to query:** OpenAlex, Google Scholar, UX industry benchmark reports (e.g., AppsFlyer, Branch, UXCam, CXL).
- **What we're looking for:** Quantitative A/B test results and retention metrics comparing upfront signup gates vs. progressive disclosure/deferred signup.

### Pillar 2: Loss Aversion & Endowment Effect in EdTech / Onboarding
- **Sources to query:** Academic literature in behavioral economics and HCI (Kahneman, Thaler, Ryan & Deci SDT, Bandura self-efficacy).
- **What we're looking for:** Empirical studies on how user-generated progress or early rewards convert into registration drivers rather than abandonment triggers.

### Pillar 3: Indonesian Teacher Digital Literacy & PMM Ecosystem
- **Sources to query:** Indonesian academic journals (Sinta-indexed), researchgate, regional university studies (Unram, UM, UNUJA, Kemendikbudristek reports).
- **What we're looking for:** Empirical TAM analyses of Platform Merdeka Mengajar (PMM), digital workload challenges, bandwidth/infrastructure barriers, and credential-driven engagement patterns among Indonesian teachers.

## Success criteria (what "done" looks like)
- A complete, structured synthesis document (`SYNTHESIS.md`) that cites exact studies, sample sizes, methodology, key findings, and actionable design rules for our Android app.
- A fully populated `sources.md` table verifying peer-reviewed papers, university repositories, and industry data with zero unverified claims.
