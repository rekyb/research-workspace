## Onboarding & activation

### Single recommended next step (goal → first meaningful action)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops` (Khan "UP NEXT FOR YOU!" on the course page and "Up next: Lesson 3" after each set — `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/07-skill-summary-mastery.png`); `research/2026-07-09-benchmark-synthesis-learn-to-hire-loop` (Pattern 1, corroborated with Brilliant's smart-search recommendation-with-rationale — `research/2026-07-02-briliant/assets/smart-search-3.png`); `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` (the *onboarding* corollary — Khan's code-first `/join` routes a linked learner straight to their assigned content, whereas an un-routed learner is dumped into the full grade-organized catalogue and must self-locate — `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/04-classcode-entry.png` vs. `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/06-catalogue-grade-organized.png`).
- **When it works:** Early drop-off starts *before* learning begins; collapsing "what do I do now?" into one obvious action lowers decision load and shortens time-to-first-practice. Strongest when the single step routes the learner *into practice*, not into another browse surface, and when the recommendation is chosen from the learner's actual mastery state. The onboarding study makes the "not another browse surface" clause concrete: routing straight to the assigned first task beats a catalogue the learner has to hunt through.
- **When it backfires:** "Recommendation theater" — if the suggested step is generic, or the system asks clarifying questions *after* recommending, the personalization claim rings hollow and trust drops. Whether the routing is truly adaptive vs. a fixed course pointer could not be confirmed from a single account, so do not oversell it as adaptivity.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/01-course-overview.png`, `research/2026-07-02-briliant/assets/smart-search-3.png`.

### Time-boxed, typed bite-sized lessons ("1 MIN")
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience` (lessons previewed as "VOCABULARY · 1 MIN · Let's go!" before entry — `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/02-vocab-presentation-video-audio.png`).
- **When it works:** When the product's thesis is short daily practice ("ten minutes a day"), naming the lesson type and setting a one-minute expectation up front keeps perceived friction below the threshold that stops a habit forming, and makes in-session drop-off legible.
- **When it backfires:** If the stated time is aspirational (a "1 MIN" lesson that actually runs several minutes), the promise erodes trust; the framing needs the real median time-to-complete to land near the label. Bite-sizing is packaging, not substance — it must wrap a real learning loop, not replace it.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/flow.gif`, `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/02-vocab-presentation-video-audio.png`.

### Deferred, "try-first" registration (prove value before the wall)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — registration timing spans a spectrum across five apps: Duolingo defers the account entirely (language pick, intake, and placement all run in a guest session; the ask surfaces only at the `/learn` home, framed as *saving progress* — `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/01-landing-value-framing.png`), Brilliant walls signup after the intake to "discover your learning plan" (loss-aversion on invested effort — `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/07-signup-wall-discover-plan.png`), and CodeSignal is the extreme cold gate (a full name/email/password form before any value — `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/codesignal/screenshots/01-signup-gate-create-account.png`), with Khan wall-first behind a role+age pre-step (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/03-signup-wall-social-email.png`). **Enriched 2026-07-16 with literature grounding** — `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature` (Feature 1) supplies the *theory and regulatory case* beneath the observed UI pattern: cross-industry mobile-onboarding benchmarks associate an upfront wall with a 25–40% initial session drop-off (directional consumer aggregate, **not** teacher-specific — `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature/sources.md`); Perceived-Ease-of-Use predicts EdTech adoption among Indonesian teachers (Megawati et al. 2024, DOI `10.19109/intelektualita.v13i1.21095`; caveat: a *mandatory*-use TAM context measuring intention/satisfaction, so a directional analogy to a voluntary funnel, not direct funnel evidence); and — the strongest, most transferable anchor — a **structural access-exclusion** argument: under KepmenPANRB No. 16/2025, post-2024 honorary teachers (`Guru Honorer`) are locked out of Dapodik and never receive the `belajar.id` credential a login wall would demand.
- **When it works:** For low-context, email-scarce, first-time audiences, every field asked before value is delivered is a drop-off point. A guest session lets motivation build before commitment is required, and reframing the eventual signup as "save what you already made" converts loss-aversion into a *reason* to register rather than a barrier. Directly attacks the email-less-learner stall and the duplicate/abandoned-account problem that forced upfront signup creates. [ref: Kahneman, Knetsch & Thaler 1991, loss aversion / endowment effect] The literature study adds a distinct, transferable rationale *beyond friction*: when the login wall depends on a credential a growing user segment **structurally cannot obtain** (post-2024 `Guru Honorer` locked out of `belajar.id`/Dapodik under KepmenPANRB 16/2025), deferring registration is an access-*inclusion* requirement, not merely a conversion optimization. Low-friction re-capture at the value moment (e.g. WhatsApp OTP / one-click login) is a *design inference* from the PEOU evidence, not itself an empirically tested mechanism.
- **When it backfires:** The named **anti-pattern is a cold account/assessment gate before value** — CodeSignal (and more mildly Khan) demand identity before showing anything, wasting even a strong placement mechanic behind a form a hesitant learner never crosses. The opposite failure is deferring so much that nothing is extracted to personalize the first task; the guest-to-registered **account-merge** is the load-bearing engineering risk (botch it and the duplicate-account pain returns in a new wrapper). Sits in direct tension with any "extract profile/level up front" model (e.g. a planned goal→level→profile→path): resolve *how much* personalization to pull before the first win vs. after. Do not over-borrow the literature's *numbers*: the 25–40% drop-off is a cross-industry consumer aggregate and the PEOU evidence comes from a *mandatory*-use civil-service context, so both justify *direction* for a voluntary teacher funnel, never a target magnitude — the teacher-specific effect is unmeasured (`research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature/SYNTHESIS.md` `## Gaps & caveats`).
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/01-landing-value-framing.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/codesignal/screenshots/01-signup-gate-create-account.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/07-signup-wall-discover-plan.png`. Caveat: desktop-web capture, single observed variant per platform; Duolingo's first *lesson* sits just beyond the captured guest session, and CodeSignal's assessment interior was left unseen by decision (not transacted).

### Endowment quality at the save-wall: a *created artifact* endows more than passive completion (design hypothesis)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed (literature-grounded); **untested design hypothesis**
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature` (Feature 2) — a *desk-literature* study (no live UI), building on the endowment effect (Litovsky, Loewenstein, Horn & Olivola 2022, *PNAS* 119(34):e2202700119, DOI `10.1073/pnas.2202700119`) and SDT autonomy-supportive framing (Silva et al. 2025, DOI `10.28945/5549`).
- **When it works:** The loss-aversion "save what you made" wall (see *Deferred, "try-first" registration* and *Three-stage UX sequencing* below) is only as strong as the endowment it protects. This study's hypothesis: a passive quiz / XP completion generates *weaker* psychological ownership than a tangible artifact the user **created** through real labor (here, a generated `Modul Ajar / RPP` lesson plan), so triggering the save-wall right after artifact creation — framed *informationally* ("save your work so it isn't lost & claim your certificate") rather than administratively ("create an account to continue") — should convert better. The loss > gain *direction* is literature-consistent (endowment theory + SDT informational framing).
- **When it backfires:** This is an **untested design hypothesis, not a demonstrated result** — no cited source establishes that a created-artifact endowment beats quiz/XP completion; validate with an A/B (gain-framed vs. loss-framed post-artifact modal) before relying on it. The loss-aversion magnitude ($\lambda \approx 2.2$) is **illustrative and actively contested** (2024 meta-analysis $\lambda \approx 1.955$, high heterogeneity; 2025 robustness challenge), so it indicates *direction only*, never a transferable conversion multiplier; any "≥18% lift" figure is an illustrative placeholder, not evidence-derived. Controlling / surveillance framing negates the SDT benefit. **Refines** the *Three-stage UX sequencing* Stage-3 example (which reframes the wall around saving "XP and personalized plan"): the hypothesis predicts a *created artifact* would endow more strongly than XP — a refinement to test, not a settled contradiction of that benchmark-observed pattern.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature/SYNTHESIS.md` (Feature 2 + `## Peer Review`), `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature/sources.md`.

### Single unambiguous CTA on the value-framing landing
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — Duolingo's landing is near-empty: one benefit headline, one dominant primary CTA ("Get started"), one de-emphasized "I already have an account", nothing competing (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/01-landing-value-framing.png`). Contrast: Khan leads with a role chooser (Student / Family / Teacher) as the primary action, front-loading an identity decision before any value (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/01-landing-role-split.png`); Elsa proves value on the page itself with an embedded working test (see "Assessment-as-onboarding" below).
- **When it works:** A single high-contrast CTA with no competing elements removes ambiguity about what to do next, which is critical for low-context and low-tech-literacy users who will not hunt for the right control. Directly addresses the "primary CTA mistaken for an ad/banner" problem: when the button is the only salient action, it cannot read as a promo. [ref: Hick 1952, choice reaction time]
- **When it backfires:** When the first screen instead asks for a decision (role) or competes with marketing promos, the intended action loses salience and reads as marketing rather than a button to press. A single-CTA layout also assumes one clear primary audience; genuinely distinct entry paths (learner vs. teacher/admin) may need routing, but that routing should not be the *first* thing the value-seeker meets.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/01-landing-value-framing.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/01-landing-role-split.png`. Caveat: desktop-web, single observed variant (onboarding is heavily A/B-tested).

### Optional, positively-framed placement fork (novice and advanced from one screen)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — Duolingo offers two side-by-side choices, "Start from scratch (take the easiest lesson)" and "Find my level (let Duo recommend where you should start)": placement is an opt-in fork, never a gate (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/08-choose-path-fork.png`). Brilliant goes further with autonomy support: after an advanced self-pick (Calculus) it *recommends* a foundation course but still lets the user jump straight to the hard content (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/06-start-point-recommended.png`). (Pairs with the recognition-based selector below: this entry is the *framing/optionality*, that one is the *mechanic*.)
- **When it works:** Framing placement as "let me help you find the right start" removes the social threat of a test, so a novice is not intimidated and an advanced user is not forced through basics; serving both paths on one screen lets a single flow cover the full ability range without branching the UI. Recommending foundations while permitting the jump nudges mastery without removing control. [ref: Ryan & Deci 2000, self-determination theory / autonomy support]
- **When it backfires:** A "recommendation" the user cannot override becomes a gate again and loses the autonomy benefit. If the fork's two options are not visibly different in stakes, a novice may still fear the "Find my level" branch as a test; the positive framing has to be explicit. Mis-placement (advanced user under-placed, or novice over-placed) needs a cheap in-task correction path, since a single self-selection is a coarse signal.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/08-choose-path-fork.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/06-start-point-recommended.png`.

### Level selection by recognition, not by label
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — Brilliant's level selector shows an actual worked problem on each escalating card plus a first-person capability statement ("Arithmetic — I want to start from the basics", `5 × ½`, up to "Calculus — I understand derivatives and integrals"): the user recognizes the math they can do rather than self-rating a label (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/05-level-self-select-by-problem.png`). Duolingo runs a lighter version: a self-rated ladder rendered as signal-strength bars, "I'm new" (one bar) to "I can discuss most topics" (four bars), encoding level visually as well as verbally (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/04-proficiency-self-select.png`).
- **When it works:** Recognition is more inclusive than abstract self-assessment and lands for every user type: a low-literacy or non-native learner can identify a problem or a signal-bar level without parsing "beginner/intermediate/advanced" vocabulary, an advanced learner instantly spots their tier, and a novice is not shamed by jargon. Triple-encoding the choice (worked example + topic name + "I can…" statement) means at least one cue lands. [ref: Standing 1973, recognition-memory superiority]
- **When it backfires:** Recognition-memory research supports the *inclusivity/ease* claim; that recognition-based placement is *more accurate* than label self-rating is a **hypothesis, not a demonstrated result** — do not sell accuracy without the diagnostic-agreement study. Authoring a real worked problem per level is more content cost than a text radio list, and the exemplar problems must themselves be well-calibrated or they misplace users.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/05-level-self-select-by-problem.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/04-proficiency-self-select.png`.

### Assessment-as-onboarding, delivered before the wall
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — Elsa embeds a genuinely working speaking test on its marketing page (a sentence to pronounce, an audio model to hear, a mic to record) reachable with no signup or download (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/elsa-speak/screenshots/03-web-speaking-assessment.png`). CodeSignal is the cautionary inverse: its signature AI skill-assessment placement is identical in spirit but sits entirely behind a mandatory account form, so a prospective learner never reaches it (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/codesignal/screenshots/01-signup-gate-create-account.png`).
- **When it works:** An assessment that is also a demo lets users feel competence and see the product's value in the first minute (a strong activation + acquisition hook) and produces the placement signal for free. Sequencing it *before* the wall (Elsa) vs. behind it (CodeSignal) is the difference between "try then commit" and "commit to try" — the former fits a hesitant, low-commitment audience far better. [ref: Roediger & Karpicke 2006, testing effect — supports only that a real assessment is genuine learning, *not* the before-the-wall conversion claim, which rests on the Elsa-vs-CodeSignal contrast]
- **When it backfires:** A real scoring engine can be a **High-effort, novel-ML workstream** (Elsa's pronunciation scoring) that must not be smuggled into an MVP; decouple the *sequencing* pattern (un-gated first task) from the *scoring* build. Watch the honesty of the demo: in this capture Elsa's mic fired on a silent clip and the AI score appeared only as a marketing mockup, so the scoring was observed *as claimed, not as a returned result* — do not overclaim a live score you have not seen. Speaking assessments also need permission priming (below) before they work on real devices.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/elsa-speak/screenshots/03-web-speaking-assessment.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/codesignal/screenshots/01-signup-gate-create-account.png`. Caveat: Elsa's true onboarding is app-only (we captured the marketing site + web test + app mockups); CodeSignal's assessment interior was not entered.

### Code-first, chrome-free linked entry to assigned content
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — Khan's `/join` page is a single-task screen ("Join your class on Khan Academy") with a segmented, fixed-length code input and nothing else: no nav, no catalogue. A valid code routes the learner to their assigned class; an invalid code is rejected inline (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/04-classcode-entry.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/classcode-flow.gif`). Without a code, the learner faces the full grade-organized catalogue and must self-locate (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/06-catalogue-grade-organized.png`).
- **When it works:** For learners who arrive via a facilitator, teacher, or program link, a code-first entry collapses the hardest navigation problem (finding the right course among many) into one deterministic step. Stripping the page of all other UI keeps a low-context user focused on the single action, and a segmented input makes the code format self-evident and cuts entry errors. This is the onboarding-time expression of "route into the task, not into a browse surface" (see *Single recommended next step*). [ref: Hick 1952, choice reduction; Sweller 1988, cognitive load]
- **When it backfires:** Only pays off when program/facilitator links are a genuine acquisition channel — for pure organic self-signup there is no code to enter, so it is a conditional build, not a universal one. A chrome-free page also strands a user who arrived *without* a code and needs the catalogue, so the entry point that lands them here matters.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/04-classcode-entry.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/06-catalogue-grade-organized.png`.

### Permission priming with a no-penalty fallback
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — Duolingo primes then prompts: the mascot says "I'll remind you to practice so it becomes a habit" and *then* the browser notification prompt fires in context (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/07-notification-permission-primer.png`); its speaking tasks also degrade gracefully via a "can't speak now" escape hatch that marks the item done with no penalty and temporarily disables speaking (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/10-placement-speaking-mic-skip.png`). Elsa is the cautionary opposite: its web speaking test fires the mic on click with no rationale-first screen (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/elsa-speak/screenshots/04-mic-recording-active.png`).
- **When it works:** Users deny permissions they do not understand, and a cold system prompt (especially the OS-level mic prompt on mobile) is a common dead-end for low-tech-literacy learners who cannot recover a blocked-permission state on their own. Priming raises grant rates by supplying context immediately before the prompt, and a no-penalty fallback ensures a denied or missing permission never halts the flow. [ref: Felt et al. 2012, contextual/runtime permission requests]
- **When it backfires:** The named **anti-pattern is the un-primed hardware permission** — Elsa's cold mic-on-click (on a first-time device this is the raw OS prompt that strands users). Note the capture caveat: on this pre-granted browser profile no OS prompt actually appeared, so the *cold-prompt dead-end* is the well-established failure mode inferred for a first-time device, not something observed firing here. On-device permission handling (and the mic scoring engine behind it) is a real mobile-only risk surface the desktop-web capture could not exercise.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/07-notification-permission-primer.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/10-placement-speaking-mic-skip.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/elsa-speak/screenshots/04-mic-recording-active.png`.

### Character-guided, icon-first, low-text intake
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — all three character-led apps use a mascot as a friendly guide (Duolingo's Duo, Brilliant's Koji "I'll be your personal tutor", CodeSignal's Cosmo) and pose each question as a short prompt with pictorial choice cards (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/03-intake-why-learning.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/02-motivation-intake.png`). Brilliant extends personalization to the guide itself, letting the user choose the tutor's voice. **Corroborated 2026-07-16 by literature** — `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature` (Feature 3) *recommends* a lightweight, icon-first context selector that segments by the user's exact real-world role (`Jenjang: PAUD/SD/SMP/SMA/SMK`) and routes into short 5–15 min async modules, grounded in Indonesian infrastructure-gap research (Kurniawati 2025, DOI `10.32550/wdstp587`) — a literature *recommendation*, not an observed UI.
- **When it works:** A guide character lowers cognitive load and adds warmth, turning a form into a conversation that reassures first-time and low-context users they are being led; icon-first, low-text choices cut the reading/typing burden that blocks low-literacy and non-native learners; personalizing the guide creates early ownership that motivates completion. The **icon-first** half is the cheap, high-leverage part (standard components); the animated guide character is a costlier add-on. [ref: Lester et al. 1997, persona effect; Sweller 1988, cognitive load] Independent infrastructure/adoption research for low-bandwidth Indonesian teacher contexts corroborates the low-text, icon-first, **segment-by-exact-role** approach and a short (5–15 min) async module length as an *access requirement*, not just a nicety (`research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature` Feature 3) — the segment cue also front-loads cultural/role relevance and lowers overhead.
- **When it backfires:** A guide character carries art/animation and (if voiced or "tutor"-framed) content/ML cost, and can feel patronizing to advanced users — make it skippable/quiet for them. Icon cards only work if the icons are unambiguous; a poorly chosen pictogram is *harder* than the word. Low-text intake still has to capture enough signal to route, so it trades richness for reach.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/03-intake-why-learning.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/02-motivation-intake.png`.

### Bounded, reversible onboarding momentum scaffolding
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — Duolingo brackets its questionnaire with a progress bar and a back arrow (finite and reversible), reinforces value with an outcome projection ("That's 50 words in your first week!" — `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/06-habit-projection-50-words.png`), gives instant positive feedback on placement answers ("Nice!"), and deploys an exit-intent retention modal on mid-placement quit. Brilliant threads credibility/reassurance interstitials between questions ("You can make progress in both subjects later on"); both tease a locked feature with a concrete unlock condition ("complete 3 more lessons to start competing").
- **When it works:** A visible, *bounded* progress indicator reduces the anxiety of an open-ended form and *reversibility* lowers the stakes of each choice; outcome projections and instant positive feedback supply motivation to continue; teasing a gated feature with a clear condition converts overwhelm into a single next goal. Together they sustain a low-context user through a multi-step flow. The **bounded progress bar is the near-free win**; the trigger-driven pieces (exit-intent modal, projections) are add-ons to defer. [ref: Kivetz, Urminsky & Zheng 2006, goal-gradient; Nunes & Drèze 2006, endowed progress]
- **When it backfires:** This is a grab-bag, not one atom — decompose it and adopt piece by piece rather than shipping "momentum" wholesale. An outcome projection ("50 words") that the product does not actually deliver erodes trust like any aspirational promise. Exit-intent modals shade into dark-pattern territory if they trap rather than reassure.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/06-habit-projection-50-words.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/flow.gif`.

### Deep localization of the onboarding chrome (not just content)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` — Elsa geo-redirects to a fully localized site (Indonesian, on an `id.` subdomain) where the value proposition, feature descriptions, and CTAs are all in the learner's language, not just the course content (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/elsa-speak/screenshots/01-landing-localized-value-framing-paywall.png`).
- **When it works:** For learners studying a foreign language at low proficiency, native-language *chrome* (instructions, buttons, value framing, error messages) is the difference between comprehension and abandonment: a learner who cannot yet read the target language must still understand what to do. Auto-detection removes a manual language-picker step. Best treated as a **day-one i18n architecture decision**, since it is ruinous to retrofit. [ref: L2 reading cognitive-load / UI-translation studies]
- **When it backfires:** Localizing only the lesson content while leaving the interface in English recreates the exact comprehension wall these learners face — the half-measure looks localized but is not. Translation is an ongoing content pipeline (strings, errors, projections) that scales with every surface added; geo-redirect logic also has to be right, or it strands users in the wrong locale.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/elsa-speak/screenshots/01-landing-localized-value-framing-paywall.png`.

### Three-stage UX sequencing architecture (minimal intake → first win → contextual save wall)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` (`REVIEW.md` §4, resolving `SYNTHESIS.md` Pre-win vs. Post-win extraction tension across Duolingo, Brilliant, Khan Academy, and CodeSignal).
- **When it works:** Resolves the structural tension between needing user data (goal, level, path) to personalize the first lesson versus the high drop-off of upfront registration (`Kahneman, Knetsch & Thaler 1991`, loss aversion). Divided into three strict stages: (1) **Minimal Routing Intake (Guest Session, <45s):** Collect only Goal/Motivation + Level Selection (worked problem pick). Ask zero PII, avatar, or device permissions. (2) **First Interactive Win (Playable Task, 1–2m):** Route straight into a calibrated mini-lesson or assessment (`F5`) with progress bars (`H1`) and immediate positive feedback (`F9`). (3) **Contextual Account Wall (Post-Activation):** Reframe registration around saving earned value (`F1`): *"You mastered Level 1! Create an account to save your 50 XP and personalized plan."* Offer 1-tap social authentication (`CodeSignal H4` pattern) alongside email.
- **When it backfires:** If account merge from the guest session during Stage 3 fails or loses progress state, users lose their earned XP, destroying trust instantly. Sits in opposition to **cold account gates before value delivery** (e.g. CodeSignal upfront registration), which strand users before they experience product value.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/REVIEW.md`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/duolingo/screenshots/01-landing-value-framing.png`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/brilliant/screenshots/07-signup-wall-discover-plan.png`.

### Humanizing touches at the registration wall (signed personal commitment)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` (`SYNTHESIS.md` §F11, `REVIEW.md`; secondary practitioner study / public case studies).
- **When it works:** Placing a brief, warm, personal note (signed by a founder, teacher, or product team) right at the registration or transition wall humanizes the product and invokes social reciprocity (`Nass 1994`, Computers as Social Actors). A user feels they are interacting with human educators rather than a cold corporate form, reducing friction and anxiety at the exact moment commitment is requested.
- **When it backfires:** If the note feels overly automated or corporatized, or if it makes unfulfillable promises (e.g., "White-glove 1-on-1 support" that cannot scale), it erodes trust. Must remain authentic, scalable, and sincere.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/SYNTHESIS.md`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/REVIEW.md`.

### Contextual just-in-time education (no front-loaded tutorial tours)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps` (`SYNTHESIS.md` §F12, `REVIEW.md`).
- **When it works:** Front-loaded tooltips and multi-screen walkthroughs ("Welcome! Click here, then here, then here") impose cognitive load before the user has context or intrinsic motivation (`Sweller 1988`). Removing front-loaded tutorials and instead explaining UI tools *only right when the user encounters the task requiring them* (just-in-time / progressive disclosure) preserves attention and keeps the user in the active learning loop (`Zeigarnik 1927`).
- **When it backfires:** If complex features are hidden with zero discoverability and no contextual hint, users may never find them. Avoid complex checklist engines or multi-step tour overlays in early MVPs; rely on self-evident affordances + contextual single-tool hints.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/SYNTHESIS.md`, `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/REVIEW.md`.

## Feedback & recovery

### Feedback that teaches, not just scores (instructional feedback panel)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience` (wrong answer returns the correct sentence, replayable audio, and a plain-language rule — `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/04-incorrect-answer-feedback.png`; right and wrong taught in the *same* panel shape — `05-correct-answer-feedback.png`); `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops` (wrong answer withholds the solution and offers a step-by-step worked path plus embedded video — `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/03-incorrect-feedback.png`); `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience` (submit prints the code's actual output + a forward-looking message — `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/06-submit-success-xp-reward.png`); `research/2026-07-09-benchmark-synthesis-learn-to-hire-loop` (Pattern 4, "highest-confidence pattern in the benchmark").
- **When it works:** Making the feedback panel a teaching surface (correct form + why + how to fix) turns the moment of being graded into the moment of learning, and removes the "punished for guessing" feeling that makes learners cautious. Teaching right and wrong answers with the *same* panel shape reinforces the rule either way.
- **When it backfires:** Person-level praise copy ("Nice work! You got it") is one of the *least* effective feedback types (Hattie & Timperley 2007, cited in the Khan study); positive feedback should point at the process or the mastery gained, not the person. Rich instructional feedback is also authored, item-attached content, so it carries an ongoing authoring cost per item.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/04-incorrect-answer-feedback.png`, `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/04-graduated-hint-and-video.png`, `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/06-submit-success-xp-reward.png`.

### Productive-struggle remediation (withhold the answer, give graduated hints)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops` (a miss yields "Not quite yet… give it another try" with "Show a step" revealed one step at a time + a related teaching video, never the answer — `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/04-graduated-hint-and-video.png`); `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience` (tiered Take Hint → Show Answer — `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/05-hint-scaffolding-xp-cost.png`); `research/2026-07-09-benchmark-synthesis-learn-to-hire-loop` (Pattern 3; Brilliant's Koji companion is positioned in-lesson but its full tutor behaviour was not observable — `research/2026-07-02-briliant/assets/ai-companion-1.png`).
- **When it works:** Being stuck with no path forward is the number-one churn moment in self-paced learning. Withholding the answer preserves the retrieval attempt (the part that builds memory) while graduated hints keep cognitive load manageable and keep the learner *in the task* instead of tab-switching to Google.
- **When it backfires:** Help titration is the hard part — learners can use hints as an answer-shortcut and clear tasks without learning (watch over-reliance as the failure mode). Authoring graduated hints + a mapped video per item is a content pipeline from zero that scales linearly and never ends.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/03-incorrect-feedback.png`, `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/05-hint-scaffolding-xp-cost.png`.

### Priced help (hints cost effort-currency)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience` (Take Hint −30 XP lowers the exercise reward 100 → 70; Show Answer −70 XP forfeits the rest; each hint asks "Was this helpful? Yes/No" — `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/05-hint-scaffolding-xp-cost.png`).
- **When it works:** Charging the reward currency for help protects the "desirable difficulty" that makes learning stick — help is available but not free, so learners attempt first. The Yes/No prompt doubles as a hint-quality signal.
- **When it backfires:** If the price reads as punitive rather than a nudge, it can suppress help-seeking exactly when a learner needs it; test the copy/affordance so the trade-off is understood before the click. Pricing help only makes sense once an effort-currency (XP) already exists and is credible.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/05-hint-scaffolding-xp-cost.png`.

### Front-loaded real spoken/modelled input before practice
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience` (new vocabulary introduced as native-speaker video with replayable audio and a save-word bookmark, before the practice step — `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/02-vocab-presentation-video-audio.png`).
- **When it works:** For skills where modality matters (language listening/pronunciation), modelling the target with real audio/video from the first step trains perception that text alone cannot, and gives the learner something concrete to imitate before they produce.
- **When it backfires:** Video/audio production is expensive to author and maintain, and the modality advantage is domain-specific (it buys less in text-only subjects). Effectiveness of a "speed control" or replay is only worth claiming to the extent it is actually observed (Busuu's was seen at 1× only).
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/02-vocab-presentation-video-audio.png`, `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/flow.md`.

## Mastery & progress signaling

### Mastery signal tied to accuracy, not completion (the progress bar *is* the proficiency estimate)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops` (per-skill mastery levels Not started → Familiar → Proficient → Mastered shown as a colour grid + mastery points, *gated on accuracy*: a two-mistake modal caps the attempt at "can no longer reach Proficient," so 5/7 earns Familiar, not Proficient — `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/06-mastery-gate-modal.png`, `07-skill-summary-mastery.png`); `research/2026-07-09-benchmark-synthesis-learn-to-hire-loop` (Pattern 5, "mastery signals that mean something").
- **When it works:** Collapsing "what you've learned" and "your progress bar" into one accuracy-driven object makes the learner optimise the right thing — the reward *is* the proficiency state — and gives an honest self-assessment surface. The accuracy gate is the cheap mechanism that makes the expensive mastery signal trustworthy and stops grinding to a hollow badge.
- **When it backfires:** Done without a calibrated item bank / estimator, it is "a completion bar in a proficiency costume." Platform mastery is *near-transfer* (doing the platform's items), not demonstrated far transfer, so it should not be sold as proven real-world ability without delayed/transfer validation.
- **⚠️ Contradiction (flagged 2026-07-13):** Khan subordinates the visible progress signal to accuracy, whereas DataCamp (XP) and Busuu (Stars/Score) award their headline progress on *completion/activity*, which the meta-synthesis notes are "less clearly tied to mastery." Same surface ("your progress"), opposite guarantee — resolve per product: is the collected number meant to certify mastery, or just fuel habit? Do not silently treat XP-style points as a mastery signal.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/06-mastery-gate-modal.png`, `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/08-mastery-grid-after.png`.

## Motivation & retention

### Layered motivation stack (points + streak + daily challenges + time-boxed league)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience` (Stars/Score per lesson, daily streak flame, "Today's challenges" progress bars, weekly Blue League — `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/10-todays-challenges.png`); `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience` (XP, Daily Streak, weekly Bit League — `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/08-leaderboard-bit-league.png`); `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops` (energy points + streak, kept mostly separate from the learning-tied mastery scale — `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/01-course-overview.png`); `research/2026-07-09-benchmark-synthesis-learn-to-hire-loop` (Pattern 6, "return loops with guardrails").
- **When it works:** Learning fails on *consistency, not comprehension*; the stack attacks habit at three altitudes — frequent tangible progress (points), don't-break-the-chain (streak + daily goals via goal-gradient/loss-aversion), and a resetting social deadline (league) — answering "why open the app today?"
- **When it backfires:** Points/streaks are activity-tied and can reward presence over mastery ("hollow engagement"); research shows time-on-task can even move *opposite* to learning. Always pair retention metrics (D1/D7/D30) with a learning-gain/retrieval guardrail, and correlate points earned with actual learning so the currency rewards learning, not gaming.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/08-lesson-complete-stars-score.png`, `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/10-todays-challenges.png`, `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/08-leaderboard-bit-league.png`.

### Time-boxed peer league / leaderboard (resetting social competition)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience` (weekly Blue League, ranked table of ~20+ learners, own row highlighted — `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/11-blue-league-leaderboard.png`); `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience` (weekly Bit League, "Gain 250XP to enter", "3 days left" — `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/08-leaderboard-bit-league.png`).
- **When it works:** A ranked, *time-boxed* league adds social proof and a deadline that manufactures urgency, then resets — so it motivates without permanently demoralising, and needs no permanent-loss punishment.
- **When it backfires:** A **demotivation tail** for bottom-ranked learners is the named harmed segment across both studies — segment by rank and consider opt-out / cohort-banding. It also needs critical mass to populate credible cohorts (matchmaking + weekly rollover jobs), so it is a "gate on scale," not a day-one build. Third-party names on the leaderboard are a PII-redaction surface.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/11-blue-league-leaderboard.png`, `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/08-leaderboard-bit-league.png`.

### Post-task reward / celebration moment
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience` (lesson-complete screen: Stars +10, Score 80%, +3 words, confetti — `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/08-lesson-complete-stars-score.png`); `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops` (end-of-set summary: new level, "🔥 You improved your skills! 🔥", energy points, "Up next" — `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/07-skill-summary-mastery.png`); `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience` (green ✓, +XP, live counter — `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience/platforms/datacamp/screenshots/06-submit-success-xp-reward.png`).
- **When it works:** Placing the reward beat immediately after real effort, tied to an *earned* result, plus an "Up next" nudge, uses goal-gradient momentum to pull the learner into the next rep while motivation is still high.
- **When it backfires:** Expected, activity-contingent tangible rewards (points) can *crowd out* intrinsic interest (Deci, Koestner & Ryan 1999, cited in the Khan study); anchor the celebration to the *competence signal* (the mastery level) rather than the point total, or the point number becomes the headline. Two-scale mismatch is a real risk: Khan's set advanced mastery but left the weekly "Level up" meter at 0/1, which a learner reads as "I did the work but the game says 0."
- **⚠️ Contradiction (flagged 2026-07-13):** Busuu and DataCamp present the points/Stars celebration as an unqualified positive, while the Khan study — via cited motivation research — treats the same tangible-reward-after-task mechanic as a crowd-out *risk* to mitigate. Keep both readings: celebrate, but anchor to competence and guard against the reward becoming the goal.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/08-lesson-complete-stars-score.png`, `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/07-skill-summary-mastery.png`.

### Institution-recognized credentials as an activation/completion driver (and the vanity-certificate trap)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed (literature-grounded)
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature` (Feature 3) — a *desk-literature* study (no live UI): verifiable PD certificates (`Sertifikasi` / `Jam Pelatihan`) drive teacher adoption (Utomo & Lestari 2025; Setyaningrum & Susanto 2024, DOI `10.53008/kalbisocio.v11i2.4359`, single Bekasi district), against the credential-valuation framework Perdirjen GTK No. 7607/2023 (`RHK` / `e-Kinerja`).
- **When it works:** For learners inside a structured professional / civil-service framework (here, Indonesian teachers), tying module completion to a credential that carries **official, institutionally-recognized value** (PD credit counting toward `Angka Kredit` / `e-Kinerja`) is a strong *extrinsic* completion driver — the credential, not the learning UI, is the goal. The reliably shippable version is **tier-1**: an immediately exportable artifact the user files into their *own* existing workflow (a `Modul Ajar / Bukti Karya` a teacher enters in their own `RHK`), which delivers real recognized value entirely under product control while also solving a daily job-to-be-done.
- **When it backfires:** The **vanity-certificate trap** — a standalone, unaccredited in-app PDF certificate carries *zero* official valuation (Perdirjen GTK 7607/2023), so a credential that only *looks* official drives no adoption and can erode trust. The stronger "tier-2" version — formal co-certification (`MGMP/KKG 32 JP`) carrying `e-Kinerja BKN` recognition — is an **open governance hypothesis, not a shippable feature**: recognized credit flows through the user's own institutional approval (a `Kepala Sekolah`-approved `RHK` entry) and depends on locally-autonomous bodies outside product control, so realizing it is a multi-year, district-by-district policy/partnership workstream (`research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature/SYNTHESIS.md` `## Gaps & caveats`). Evidence skews to single districts (Bekasi / Bali), so the weight of credential motivation vs. infrastructure barriers may shift in remote / `3T` contexts. Generalize as: *credential recognition works only when the credential is genuinely recognized by the institution the user answers to — build the tier you actually control.* **Cross-ref:** the *Layered motivation stack* and *Post-task reward* entries caution that extrinsic tangible rewards can crowd out intrinsic interest (Deci, Koestner & Ryan 1999); a real professional credential differs from in-app points in that it is the user's genuine external career goal, but the crowd-out caution still applies to how heavily the credential is dangled over the learning itself.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature/SYNTHESIS.md` (Feature 3 + `## Gaps & caveats`), `research/2026-07-16-indonesian-teacher-onboarding-literature/sources.md`.

## Re-engagement & return

### AI-generated session recap on return (rebuild context, force light retrieval)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-datacamp-learning-experience` ("Review what you learned to go faster today" → "Your recent learnings": an AI summary of the last session with key points, a code example, and "Was this useful? ### Peer/community correction of real output (reciprocity loop)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience` (learners submit short writing/speaking and get corrected by other members framed as native speakers, and correct others in return — Discover feed + one-tap "Give correction" — `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/12-community-hub-native-speakers.png`, `13-community-give-correction-feed.png`, `14-give-correction-view.png`); `research/2026-07-09-benchmark-synthesis-learn-to-hire-loop` (Pattern 6 notes it as promising but with the receiving side and correction quality unobserved).
- **When it works:** The hardest, most-avoided part of learning is producing real output and finding out where it's wrong; routing production to human correctors approximates individualised feedback that automated grading cannot, at community scale and near-zero marginal cost. The reciprocity loop (correct others to earn corrections) supplies the correction labour *and* builds a social obligation that pulls learners back.
- **When it backfires:** It is a two-sided marketplace with cold-start liquidity risk — the make-or-break metric is submission→first-correction time; if corrections are slow or scarce, the whole feature fails. It also stacks a UGC moderation/safety surface (often minors) and can trigger exposure anxiety that suppresses beginner participation. In the study only the *give-correction* side was observed; the receiving side and the "native speaker" quality claim were not, so treat the value as unproven until the second side is instrumented.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/screenshots/14-give-correction-view.png`, `research/2026-07-03-busuu-learning-experience/platforms/busuu/notes.md`.

## Efficacy measurement

### Mastery-accrual-from-zero without a pre/post baseline (a differentiation gap)
- **Kind:** benchmark-observed
- **Where seen:** `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops` (every skill starts at "Not started"; improvement is shown as mastery accrued from zero, with *no forced pre/post diagnostic*, so the platform reports coverage + proficiency, not a normalized learning gain — `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/01-course-overview.png`).
- **When it works:** Starting from zero and letting learners optionally test out is low-friction and motivating for a broad audience — good for activation and course coverage.
- **When it backfires:** Coverage/proficiency is *not* a measured learning gain (which efficacy research treats as the credible proof of upskilling), and platform mastery is near-transfer, not demonstrated far transfer. This is recorded here as the clearest **opening for our product**: a lightweight pre/post diagnostic that reports a real, normalized gain would let us claim efficacy the benchmarked platforms cannot — but it must be de-risked first, because an upfront diagnostic adds onboarding friction that could be fatal to activation (prototype the friction before building any psychometrics).
- **⚠️ Tension / cross-reference (flagged 2026-07-13):** the onboarding-activation study (`research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps`) supplies direct evidence *for* this caveat: its headline finding is "prove value before the wall," and its cold-gate anti-pattern (CodeSignal demanding a full account form, and its assessment, before any value — `research/2026-07-13-onboarding-activation-education-apps/platforms/codesignal/screenshots/01-signup-gate-create-account.png`) shows a strong assessment/placement mechanic wasted when placed *before* the first win. An upfront efficacy diagnostic is the same shape of pre-win friction. This does not flip the guidance (a pre/post diagnostic is still the efficacy opening) but hardens the sequencing rule: if we run a baseline diagnostic, deliver a win first or fold the diagnostic *into* the first task (assessment-as-onboarding, see the onboarding section), rather than gating value behind it.
- **Evidence:** `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/platforms/khan-academy/screenshots/01-course-overview.png`, `research/2026-07-06-benchmark-learning-effectiveness-loops/SYNTHESIS.md` (Feature 7).
