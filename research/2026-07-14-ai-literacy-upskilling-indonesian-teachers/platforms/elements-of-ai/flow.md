# Elements of AI: Core learner flow (concept → exercise → certificate)

**Flow:** Learner picks a language → starts Part 1 → works chapter-by-chapter through
concept + inline exercise → completes peer-reviewed essays → earns a certificate.
**Entry:** elementsofai.com → "Start the course". **Goal:** conceptual AI literacy +
a certificate, self-paced, no math/programming.

> ⚠ **Evidence basis.** Step 1 is first-hand (landing). Steps 2–6 run inside the course
> subdomain (`course.elementsofai.com`), which is **outside our browser permissions**, so they
> are reconstructed from cited external sources (Class Central, University of Helsinki course
> unit, course summary) and labelled *external*.

1. **Land and self-select / choose a language.** The homepage states the goal ("demystify AI…
   empowered, not threatened") and offers a country/language chooser and two courses (Intro /
   Building AI). *Evidence:* `screenshots/01-positioning-demystify.png`. *Delight:* explicit
   reassurance ("no complicated math or programming required," self-paced).

--- course subdomain (steps *external*) ---

2. **Start Part 1, Chapter 1 "What is AI?"** The course opens into a chaptered structure of six
   chapters. ⟶ *external.*
3. **Read a short concept, then do an inline exercise.** Each section pairs a plain-language
   explanation with an embedded exercise (quiz/interactive), reinforcing the concept
   immediately. ⟶ *external.* *This is the concept → do loop.*
4. **Progress through chapters 2–5** (AI problem-solving → real-world AI → machine learning →
   neural networks), difficulty rising gradually; still no programming in Part 1. ⟶ *external.*
5. **Reach Chapter 6 "Implications," ethics as the destination.** Only after the technical
   grounding does the course tackle algorithmic bias, AI-generated content, privacy, and the
   future of work, assessed with **peer-reviewed short essays**. ⟶ *external.* *This late
   placement is the key sequencing signal for our synthesis.*
6. **Earn the certificate.** Completion yields a certificate (and a separate 60-minute "Get AI
   Ready" track issues an EU-AI-Act-mapped literacy certificate). ⟶ *external + homepage.*

**Where friction / delight appear:** delight is the fear-reduction framing and the gentle,
self-paced, math-free ramp; the main *friction for our use-case* is that the flow builds
*understanding of* AI, not *fluency with* AI tools, and its ethics payoff comes only at the very
end. Format is text+exercise (low-bandwidth friendly) and multilingual, though Bahasa Indonesia
coverage is unconfirmed.
