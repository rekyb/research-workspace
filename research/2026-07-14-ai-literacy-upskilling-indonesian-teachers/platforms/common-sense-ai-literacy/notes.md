# Common Sense AI Literacy: Observations & Patterns

**Platform type:** Free classroom **curriculum** + teacher PD (nonprofit Common Sense Education).
**Tier:** Secondary, chosen for **RQ5 (classroom transfer)** and secondarily **RQ4 / RQ6**.
**Capture method:** Public curriculum pages captured/read first-hand; no login; no payment.
**Date captured:** 2026-07-15.

---

## Why it matters to us
This is the clearest model of the **second half of our goal**, what a fluent teacher actually
*delivers to students*. It is a **ready-made, free, grab-and-go lesson library** with an explicit
scope, so it shows the shape of a classroom-transfer kit we'd want to produce.

## RQ5 (classroom transfer): a concrete, sequenced lesson set
The **"AI Literacy Lessons for Grades 6–12"** collection is **9 quick lessons (15–20 min, one
50-min)**, each a self-contained "grab-and-go" unit (`01-ai-literacy-lessons-collection.png`):

1. What Is AI? *(video)* · 2. How Is AI Trained? · 3. AI Chatbots: Who's Behind the Screen?
*(video)* · 4. AI Chatbots & Friendship *(Dilemma Discussion)* · 5. Understanding AI Bias ·
6. How AI Bias Impacts Our Lives *(Dilemma Discussion)* · 7. AI Algorithms: How Well Do They
Know You? *(video)* · 8. Facing Off with Facial Recognition *(Dilemma Discussion)* ·
9. **Artificial Intelligence: Is It Plagiarism?** *(Dilemma Discussion + Podcast, gr. 9–12)*.

Stated student outcomes: *understand what AI is and how it works; weigh its benefits and risks;
think critically about being responsible and ethical users of AI.* Part of a larger **~150-lesson
Digital Literacy & Well-Being curriculum with a recommended per-grade scope & sequence, 100%
free** (⟶ *external, Common Sense press/curriculum pages*).

## Patterns worth stealing
1. **"Grab-and-go" 15–20-min lessons:** the exact altitude for a time-poor teacher to run
   tomorrow; our classroom-transfer kit should ship at this size, not as a semester unit.
2. **"Dilemma Discussion" format:** a structured ethical-debate lesson type. Lesson 9
   *"Is It Plagiarism?"* is a packaged answer to the academic-integrity question our brief
   centres on. **Directly reusable model** for our Module 5/6 "when is using AI cheating?"
   discussion.
3. **Concept → risk → responsibility arc** across the 9 lessons (what AI is → how it's trained →
   bias → algorithms → integrity): a coherent student-facing sequence we can adapt.
4. **Teacher PD paired with the student lessons:** a free self-paced **"AI Basics for K–12
   Teachers"** course covers foundational literacy, data-privacy safety, and ethics, so the
   teacher is prepared before delivering. Mirrors our "fluency then teach" spine (RQ6).

## RQ4 (responsible use)
Ethics is the *spine*, not an add-on: bias (2 lessons), privacy/facial recognition, chatbot
manipulation, and plagiarism are the core topics. This is the "teach students proper use" content
expressed as a lesson set rather than a policy.

## RQ7 (context-fit)
US-centric and English-first; a **UK localization exists** (Years 7–13), which shows the model
*can* be localized, but no Bahasa Indonesia version is evident. Text/video lesson format is
moderately bandwidth-heavy (videos), a caveat for low-connectivity Indonesian classrooms.

## Gaps / caveats
- Individual lesson *interiors* (slides/handouts) may require a free educator account to download;
  not captured (guardrail: no account). The collection-level scope and formats are first-hand.
- Aimed at **grades 6–12 students**, not adult teachers, so it informs the *transfer* strand, not
  the teacher-fluency strand.
