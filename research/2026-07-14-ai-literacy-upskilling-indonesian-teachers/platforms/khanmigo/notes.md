# Khanmigo (Khan Academy): Observations & Patterns

**Platform type:** AI teaching assistant (teachers, free) + Socratic AI tutor (learners/parents, paid).
**Capture method:** Public marketing captured first-hand (`screenshots/`); teacher-tool detail
and the learner tutor loop (paywalled) drawn from cited external sources (⟶ *external*). No
account created; no paid flow entered (learner/parent tier is a paid subscription; guardrail).
**Tier:** Core. **Date captured:** 2026-07-15.

---

## Positioning
Khanmigo frames itself as **"your always-available tutor / teaching assistant,"** from the
nonprofit **Khan Academy**, "designed by teachers to make your role less about busy work"
(⟶ *external, Khan support*). Three audiences are split on the homepage (**Teachers /
Learners / Parents**, plus a Writing Coach and Districts), each with its own promise
(`01-audiences-and-press.png`). Trust leans on brand and press: **WSJ, Washington Post, and a
Common Sense Media 4-star rating** are shown as social proof, not compliance badges (contrast
MagicSchool's badge wall).

## RQ1 (first win) + RQ3 (personal fluency): chat-based, "minimal prompting"
- The teacher side is **"Experience the best AI for teachers … Knock something off your to-do
  list in minutes,"** with tool cards like **Exit tickets** ("create end-of-lesson
  assessments") and **Lesson plan** surfaced in-frame (`02-for-teachers-tools.png`).
- ⟶ *external:* **25+ teacher activities**: differentiation, lesson plans, quiz questions,
  student groupings, hooks, exit tickets, rubrics, discussion prompts, letters of
  recommendation. It works in a **chat window** but is tuned so teachers *"get good results
  quickly with minimal prompting"*; the **Rubric Generator** lets you pick 3/4/5-point scales
  and suggests performance indicators (Khan support, Butte TMI).
- **Contrast with MagicSchool (important design fork):** MagicSchool removes prompting entirely
  (fill a form → draft). Khanmigo keeps a **chat** but lowers the prompting burden and adds
  structure (activities, standards alignment). Two viable answers to the same "don't make
  teachers learn prompt engineering" problem: *form-first* vs *guided-chat*. Our course should
  choose deliberately, or teach the chat model since it transfers better to general AI use.
- A differentiator: Khanmigo lesson planning is **standards-aligned and tied to Khan Academy's
  content library**, and gives an **on-demand summary of recent student work** ⟶ *external*.

## RQ4 + RQ5 (responsible use + classroom transfer): the Socratic tutor is the headline pattern
This is Khanmigo's signature and its biggest lesson for the "teach students proper AI use" half
of our goal.

- The learner promise is explicit: **"Khanmigo challenges you to think critically and solve
  problems *without giving you direct answers*"** (`03-for-learners-socratic.png`). The parent
  lane echoes it: **"Like a good tutor, Khanmigo gently guides your child to discover the
  answers themselves,"** illustrated by a *"Tutor Me"* chat that responds *"Let's solve it
  together"* rather than handing over the answer (`04-for-parents-tutor.png`).
- **Why this matters:** the single biggest fear in our brief ("teach students to use AI
  *properly*") is AI as a cheating/answer-vending machine. Khanmigo's product *is* the
  counter-model: an AI deliberately engineered to withhold the answer and coach the thinking.
  A Washington Post pull-quote notes students "posing more questions to Khanmigo than they
  might typically ask." This is the academic-integrity stance expressed as interaction design,
  not policy text.
- **Transfer implication:** to teach proper use, don't just lecture ethics; give students an AI
  that *models* proper use (Socratic, answer-withholding). Our course's classroom-transfer kit
  could specify "use an answer-withholding tutor mode" as the safe student surface.

## RQ6 (credential / motivation)
No certification/badge ladder is surfaced on the marketing site (unlike MagicSchool). Khanmigo's
pull is utility + the Khan Academy brand, plus a separate **Khan for Educators** training area
(⟶ *external, khanacademy.org/khan-for-educators*) with walkthrough videos. Weaker on the
credentialing lever we care about for Indonesian PD-hours.

## RQ2 (micro-learning / habit)
Like MagicSchool, the teacher side is utility-driven, not a gamified loop. The *learner* side,
being tutoring, is session-based. No streak mechanic observed.

## RQ7 (context-fit for Indonesia): a real positive
- ⟶ *external:* a **June 2025 Microsoft partnership** extended **free Khanmigo teacher access
  worldwide across 44 countries**, scaling on Azure (previously US-only). This materially
  improves context-fit: the teacher tier is free and internationally available, and Khan
  Academy already localizes content. Whether Indonesia/Bahasa is fully covered is unconfirmed
  and worth checking. The **learner/parent tier remains paid** (~US$4/mo per FAQ), which is a
  barrier for Indonesian families and reinforces designing the *teacher-mediated* path.
- Still English-first and desktop-web-centric in these captures; no mobile-first or Bahasa
  onboarding evidence.

## Transferable patterns
1. **Socratic, answer-withholding tutor:** the product-level answer to "AI without cheating";
   the strongest model for the teach-students half of our goal.
2. **Guided chat with minimal prompting + structured activities:** an alternative to
   MagicSchool's form-first; keeps a chat (more transferable skill) but scaffolds it.
3. **Standards-alignment + student-work summaries:** ties AI output to curriculum and to real
   student data, increasing trust and relevance.
4. **Brand/press trust** rather than compliance badges: a different trust strategy (works
   because Khan Academy is already trusted; we have no such brand, so badges may suit us more).

## Gaps / caveats
- Teacher in-app loop and the learner Socratic loop are **not first-hand captured** (chat tool
  requires login; learner tier is paid). Detail is attributed to cited external sources.
- Testimonial carousel frame captured but **excluded from committed stills** (real named
  educators + photos + an X handle → PII guardrail).
- Indonesia/Bahasa coverage of the "worldwide free" teacher tier is unconfirmed.
