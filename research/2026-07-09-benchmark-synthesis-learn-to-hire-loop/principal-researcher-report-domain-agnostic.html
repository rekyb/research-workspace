Principal Researcher's Core Strategic Thesis (Domain-Agnostic Focus)

The "Engagement vs. Evidence" Tension: Traditional EdTech platforms (Duolingo, DataCamp, standard Khan Academy) survive on daily active users (DAU) and subscription retention. Therefore, their UX patterns frequently reward effort and attendance (XP, streaks, completion badges) rather than mastery. In a "learn-to-hire" model, however, rewarding unverified completion is toxic—it inflates learner resumes and destroys employer trust.

Our Build Imperative (Agnostic V1): solve.education must adapt the friction-reducing entry points of Brilliant (`Smart Search`) and DataCamp (`Split-Screen Interactive Workspace`), paired strictly with the instructional feedback rigor of Busuu (`Rule-based Correction Panel`) and the psychometric gate of Khan Academy (`Accuracy-Gated Leveling`). To ensure broad applicability across high-value employment tracks without requiring technical or programming content at initial release, our core architecture (`FR1`–`FR8`) is built as a Proof-of-Skill Platform. Every practice session generates an auditable telemetry trail verifying independent capability in analytical reasoning, quantitative literacy, business operations, or professional communication.

The 6-Stage Domain-Agnostic Learn-to-Hire Loop Architecture

How world-class cognitive mechanics map directly to verification-ready competency signals across any domain.

01 / ACTIVATE

Guided Start to First Meaningful Action

Collapses cold-start paralysis by translating ambiguous learner goals ("I want to master Data & Quantitative Reasoning") into a single, concrete, personalized starting task with transparent pedagogical rationale.

Core Mechanism: Removing Confusion & Choice Overload Evidence: Brilliant Smart Search (Row 1), Khan Mastery Summary (Row 12).
02 / PRACTICE

Zero-Setup Interactive Production

Places the learner immediately inside a zero-setup interactive workspace (direct manipulation canvas, structured spreadsheet modeler, or interactive case solver) where they produce observable artifacts rather than passively consuming video or text.

Core Mechanism: Bjork's Active Retrieval & Generation Evidence: DataCamp Split-Screen (Row 4), Busuu 1-Min Tasks (Row 7), Brilliant Block Coding (Row 3).
03 / RECOVER

In-Context Stuck-Point Remediation

Prevents abandonment during productive struggle by offering tiered, effort-priced scaffolding (graduated hints, AI coaching) that unblocks the learner without completing the task for them.

Core Mechanism: Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development Evidence: Khan Graduated Hints (Row 11), DataCamp Take Hint (Row 5), Brilliant Koji (Row 2).
04 / CORRECT

Instructional Teaching Feedback

Transforms the moment of grading into the primary teaching surface. Wrong answers return exact diagnostic rules, native audio modeling, or step-by-step worked solutions immediately.

Core Mechanism: Hattie & Timperley's Task-Level Feedback Evidence: Busuu Correction Panel (Row 7), Khan Worked Solutions (Row 11).
05 / PROVE

Accuracy-Gated Mastery Verification

Subordinates progress leveling directly to low-error independent performance. Blocks advancement when error counts exceed thresholds, demanding consolidation over grinding.

Core Mechanism: true skill tracking & Mastery Gate Evidence: Khan Two-Mistake Gate (Row 10), Khan Color Grid (Row 9).
06 / RETURN

Competence-Anchored Retention Mechanics

Levers behavioral loss aversion and social accountability to pull learners back, using AI-generated contextual recaps and peer correction loops that refresh memory traces.

Core Mechanism: Ebbinghaus Spaced Retrieval & Social Reciprocity Evidence: DataCamp AI Recap (Row 6), Busuu Community Corrections (Row 8).

1. Brilliant — Smart Search & AI Goal Recommender

Brilliant Stage 01: Activate Priority: P1 Prototype

Empirical Description (CSV Row 1)

A natural-language input box ("Reply here…" placeholder, magnifier icon, "Ask" button) that takes a free-text goal and returns a conversational recommendation powered by AI. The card explains its reasoning in plain language ("Taking the First Steps") and points directly to a specific course topic.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

Self-directed STEM learners suffer acute choice overload and initiation paralysis when confronting broad catalogs. By prompting users for open-ended prose and returning an explicitly rationalized recommendation card, Brilliant collapses a 50-option catalog into a single highly salient action. Specifically, providing explicit causal transparency ("telling the learner why, not just what") shifts the user's locus of evaluation from skepticism to relational trust.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • Recommendation Theater & Anchoring Bias: If every novice input systematically maps to the exact same foundational module ("Taking the First Steps"), the "smart AI" framing erodes credibility when users experiment with edge queries.
  • Metaphor Collision: Combining a search magnifier icon, an "Ask" button, and a "Reply here…" placeholder blurs three distinct mental models (keyword indexing, Q&A assistant, and conversational chat), causing input hesitation.
  • Handoff Friction: If clicking the recommendation drops the learner onto a marketing course overview page rather than directly starting Exercise 1, the momentum gain of the recommender is dissipated.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Implement an AI Goal Router during onboarding. When learners state job targets (e.g., "Junior Data Analyst"), route them to one diagnostic practice check with clear rationale, bypassing catalog browsing.

Required Experimental Validation

A/B test recommender vs. persona-picker baseline. Primary metric: First-lesson start rate within 180s. Guardrail: 7-day retention (check if anchoring causes early mismatch churn).

2. Brilliant — Koji, the In-Course AI Learning Companion

Brilliant Stage 03: Recover Priority: P1 Prototype

Empirical Description (CSV Row 2)

Brilliant's in-course AI tutor appears as a small green character rendered in speech bubbles beside lesson content, paired with a "How can I help?" input accepting both typed questions and voice (mic icon, ElevenLabs spoken layer). Koji offers proactive first lines and aims to "ask, don't tell" to guide learners through mathematical and coding dead-ends.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

Koji operationalises Step-by-Step Guidance Just When Needed. The learners who most require remediation are historically the least likely to proactively invoke a help button due to ego threat. By presenting an in-context character that offers proactive, low-stakes conversational cues ("Looks like you're exploring loops... need a hint?"), Koji lowers the metacognitive threshold for asking assistance without detaching the learner from the underlying workspace.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • The Paywall Conversion vs. Pedagogy Conflict: Koji is Brilliant's primary Premium monetization lever. For free tiers, Koji deliberately cuts off or blocks assistance right at the moment of cognitive impasse to force a paywall popup. This subordinates instructional recovery to conversion mechanics.
  • LLM Hallucination & Code Fence Exposure: While Brilliant guarantees Koji "won't make a math mistake" by reading structured math components, open-ended coding tasks lack rigid formal verification, exposing novices to subtle AI syntax hallucinations.
  • Split-Attention Contention: On smaller viewports, Koji's large chat panel pushes primary problem components off-screen, violating Sweller's spatial contiguity principle and escalating working memory load.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Deploy a task-aware AI assistant restricted to Socratic hints ("What happens to the calculation if parameter X is doubled in step 2?"). Never permit the AI to directly output complete solutions or final answers.

Required Experimental Validation

Holdout study comparing heavy AI hint users vs. unaided control on a 48-hour delayed transfer quiz. Ensure AI scaffolding does not inflate immediate task completion at the expense of retrieval strength.

3. Brilliant — Interactive Non-MCQ Block Sequencing Canvas

Brilliant Stage 02: Practice Priority: P1 Adapt Now

Empirical Description (CSV Row 3)

A drag-to-sequence coding exercise (Scratch/Blockly lineage) where learners fill two program slots with command blocks to drive a truck and deliver a package, then hit "Check". Dashed drop-zones signal affordance, and "Start over" sits prominently for low-cost resets.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

Direct manipulation of visual command blocks isolates computational logic, control flow, and decomposition from syntax friction (missing semicolons, indentation errors). By representing world state visually (a truck moving across grid coordinates), the learner engages in continuous mental model testing. The cheap, prominent "Start over" button systematically lowers the affective cost of failure, encouraging rapid iterative hypothesis testing.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • The Abstraction Transfer Wall: Cognitive science literature confirms that learners who master visual block dragging or guided sequencing frequently hit an abstraction wall when transitioning to unassisted open-ended task production. Sequencing proves logic recognition, not independent creation capability.
  • Binary Error Truncation: If hitting "Check" on an incorrect sequence merely displays a static "Incorrect" modal instead of animating or showing exactly where the logic fails, the interactive canvas degrades into a glorified multiple-choice question.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Use interactive block sequencing and drag-to-order mechanics for initial conceptual diagnostic checks across any domain, requiring a rigorous unassisted production capstone before issuing credentials.

Required Experimental Validation

Measure transfer performance: evaluate whether learners who complete 10 sequencing exercises perform significantly better on their first open-ended unassisted task than text-only peers.

4. DataCamp — Split-Screen In-Browser Code Editor & Live Shell

DataCamp Stage 02: Practice Priority: P0 Core Architecture

Empirical Description (CSV Row 4)

A split-screen player pairing step-by-step instructions with a real, zero-setup in-browser code editor (`script.py`) and live working IPython shell. The code editor is pre-seeded with scaffolded structure and blanks (`____`). On clicking Submit Answer, code execution is graded instantly, awarding +70 XP and live top-bar incrementing.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

This layout represents the gold standard for **authentic task simulation without environment friction**. Eliminating local Python/API key setup removes the single highest-dropout barrier in technical education. By combining **fill-in-the-blank scaffolding** with a functional REPL, the architecture calibrates cognitive load precisely into the *desirable difficulty / productive struggle* zone—preventing blank-page paralysis while requiring genuine syntactic recall.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • Pattern Matching Over Comprehension: When blanks (`____`) only require single-token insertions (e.g., typing `import` or `.head()`), learners can guess correct tokens via syntactic pattern matching without understanding underlying data structures.
  • The Local Environment Cliff: Because DataCamp abstracts away virtual environments, package installations (`pip`), and terminal debugging, learners who clear advanced courses routinely experience severe shock and failure when asked to run scripts from a real local CLI.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Adapt as `solve.education`'s core Split-Screen Practice Workspace (`FR2`). Provide a split-screen canvas where learners manipulate structured tables, financial models, or analytical case solvers with zero setup latency. Fade scaffolding progressively across milestones from fill-in-the-blank prompts to unassisted open-ended capstones.

Required Experimental Validation

Evaluate fading efficacy: compare graduation pass rates of guided fill-in cohorts against progressively un-scaffolded cohorts on an unaided open-ended capstone build.

5. DataCamp — Effort-Priced Progressive Hints & AI Helper

DataCamp Stage 03: Recover Priority: P1 Prototype

Empirical Description (CSV Row 5)

Each exercise features a "Take Hint" control (−30 XP) revealing concrete guidance ("Use `OpenAI()` to create the client…"). A second tier, "Show Answer" (−70 XP), forfeits remaining XP. An optional AI Tutor sits alongside for open-ended conversational debugging.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

DataCamp brilliantly operationalises effort pricing through behavioral neuroeconomics. By attaching a concrete currency penalty (−30 XP) to hint access and full forfeiture (−70 XP) to answer reveals, the interface inserts a metacognitive speed bump. This protects Bjork's desirable difficulty: learners pause and expend genuine internal retrieval effort before choosing to purchase external cognitive relief.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • XP Inflation vs. Audit Integrity: If XP is purely a cosmetic gamification token without real-world utility, learners quickly become insensitive to the penalty—clicking "Show Answer" reflexively to clear difficult exercises and bypassing learning entirely.
  • Punitive Frustration: For highly conscientious learners, deducting XP for taking a legitimate, well-structured educational hint can feel emotionally punitive, discouraging healthy self-regulated help-seeking.
Strategic Application for solve.education

In our verification architecture, do not deduct points; instead, log `Help_Tier_Invocations` into the cryptographic learner transcript. If a learner solves a task with Tier 1 Hints, flag the skill badge as `Scaffolded Mastery`; if solved unaided, flag as `Independent Mastery`.

Required Experimental Validation

Instrument the hint escalation funnel: measure what percentage of learners who take Hint 1 escalate immediately to Show Answer vs. successfully self-correcting their code.

6. DataCamp — AI-Powered Resumption Recap & Bit League Leaderboards

DataCamp Stage 06: Return Priority: P2 Monitor

Empirical Description (CSV Row 6)

A multi-layered retention engine featuring daily streak counters, weekly time-boxed Bit League leaderboards (ranked among peers on comparable XP), and an AI-generated session resumption modal ("Review what you learned to go faster today... When you left 15 hours ago, you worked on...").

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

This feature pair attacks both **affective loss aversion** (streaks/leagues) and **cognitive context decay** (the AI recap). Multi-session adult learners suffer severe memory trace decay (`Ebbinghaus forgetting curve`) when returning after 24–48 hours. By presenting a concise AI recap of exact code concepts practiced prior to session close, DataCamp forces light retrieval priming and lowers the cognitive switching cost of re-entering a complex coding curriculum.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • Leaderboard Demotivation & Toxic Gamification: Psychometric literature proves that competitive leaderboards systematically demotivate learners in the bottom 40% of the distribution, triggering learned helplessness and accelerated churn.
  • Goodhart's Law & XP Gaming: When learners compete for league promotion, their behavior optimizes entirely for rapid XP harvesting—selecting trivially easy warm-up exercises or rushing through hints rather than engaging in deep conceptual consolidation.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Implement the AI Session Resumption Recap (`FR7`) to re-orient learners when they return, but completely strip out public XP leaderboards. Replace competitive rankings with personal `Skill Velocity and Accuracy Delta` charts.

Required Experimental Validation

A/B test the AI Recap modal at session resume. Measure: (1) Time taken to complete the first post-resume task, and (2) Error rate on the first task compared to non-recap control.

7. Busuu — 1-Minute Tasks with Rule-Based Instructional Feedback

Busuu Stage 04: Correct Priority: P0 Adapt Now

Empirical Description (CSV Row 7)

One-minute lessons alternating video modeling with active practice (fill-in-the-blank, word tile matching with keyboard shortcut numbers). Crucially, whenever a learner submits an incorrect tile, the wrong tile turns red and immediately slides up a consistent instructional panel containing: (1) the correct sentence, (2) replayable native audio, and (3) a concise, plain-language grammar rule ("We use 'What's your name?' to ask someone for their name").

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

In my assessment as Principal Researcher, **this is the single highest-confidence pedagogical mechanism in the entire benchmark**. Busuu completely eliminating the destructive "score-and-punish" model. By converting every error state into an immediate, localized, rule-explaining diagnostic panel (`Hattie & Timperley's Feedback About the Task and Process`), the interface transforms the exact moment of failure into the highest-leverage learning event of the session.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • Passive Dismissal & Click-Through Habituation: Because the "Continue" button appears immediately inside the correction panel, rushed learners can easily click through the grammar rule in 300 milliseconds without encoding the explanation into working memory.
  • Lack of Immediate Forced Correction: Busuu presents the correct rule and then moves the learner forward to the next item rather than requiring the learner to immediately re-type or re-assemble the corrected sentence before advancing.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Implement the Diagnostic Rule Panel (`FR3`) across all tasks. When a learner gets a task wrong, display the exact causal rule—then **require them to re-execute the task correctly before they are permitted to advance**.

Required Experimental Validation

A/B test Busuu's explanatory diagnostic panel vs. standard bare red-border error markers. Primary metric: 48-hour delayed retention accuracy on isomorphic problem structures.

8. Busuu — Community Human Peer Correction & Reciprocal Review

Busuu Stage 06: Return & Skill Proof Priority: P2 Advanced Architecture

Empirical Description (CSV Row 8)

A social layer where learners submit open-ended writing and speaking prompts that are corrected by native speakers in the community. The "Discover" feed presents peers' attempts with a prominent "Give correction" button. A reciprocity loop incentivizes correcting others to earn faster corrections on one's own submissions.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

Automated syntax checkers cannot evaluate **natural domain idiomaticity, architectural elegance, or communication clarity**. Busuu solves the 1:1 scaling bottleneck through **asynchronous social reciprocity and peer evaluation**. Receiving a detailed correction from a human peer acts as a uniquely powerful, non-generic re-engagement trigger ("Someone corrected your work") that drives organic session return.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • Correction Latency & Supply/Demand Collapse: If the ratio of active reviewers to submitting novices falls below threshold, learners wait 48+ hours for feedback—by which point the cognitive context of the prompt has entirely decayed.
  • Uncalibrated Peer Quality: Untrained peers frequently correct style preferences rather than actual errors, or rewrite the submission completely without explaining the grammatical rationale, creating severe noise in the learning signal.
Strategic Application for solve.education

In our "learn-to-hire" loop, build a **Verified Employer / Expert Review Queue (`FR8`)**. Instead of crowdsourcing to unverified peers, allow verified alumni or industry evaluators to asynchronously review open-ended capstone submissions against structured competency rubrics across any domain.

Required Experimental Validation

Evaluate human expert review vs. AI review: compare accuracy, explanation helpfulness ratings, and turnaround time of human expert evaluations against automated rubric grading.

9. Khan Academy — Discrete Mastery-State Model & Visual Color Grid

Khan Academy Stage 05: Prove Priority: P0 Core Architecture

Empirical Description (CSV Row 9)

Every skill carries a discrete mastery level (`Not Started` → `Attempted` → `Familiar` → `Proficient` → `Mastered`), represented by color blocks in a unit grid and rolled up into "mastery points" and a course-mastery percentage. Completing one 7-problem set with high accuracy transitions a skill from Not Started directly to Familiar (71%).

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

This is the definitive structural implementation of **Bloom's Mastery Learning & true skill tracking**. By collapsing "what you have completed" and "what you actually know" into one unified visual state, Khan Academy resolves the destructive tension between attendance metrics and real proficiency. The learner optimizes directly for the proficiency state because the progress grid is an honest psychometric reflection of demonstrated capability.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • The Assessment Fidelity Bottleneck: If the underlying 7-problem set consists of shallow multiple-choice items or predictable algorithmic number-swapping, the "Proficient" badge represents mastery of the specific quiz format rather than real-world domain transfer.
  • Opacity of Decay Mechanics: If mastery states remain permanently locked at "Proficient" indefinitely without time-decay degradation (`Ebbinghaus retention loss`), the dashboard overstates historic competency over multi-month gaps.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Adopt Khan's 5-tier discrete mastery schema (`FR5`). Make this exact color grid the primary artifact visible on a learner's public **Employer Verification Portfolio**, directly replacing generic resume bullet points.

Required Experimental Validation

Real-world job readiness check: correlate `solve.education`'s in-app "Proficient" level across 500 learners with their actual pass rates on unassisted employer technical case screens across target domains.

10. Khan Academy — Accuracy-Gated Progression (The Two-Mistake Gate)

Khan Academy Stage 05: Prove Priority: P0 Core Architecture

Empirical Description (CSV Row 10)

The mastery level earned in a set is hard-capped by error count. Upon registering a second mistake in a 7-problem set, a modal fires: *"You can no longer reach 'Proficient' on this attempt… Start over is available after two mistakes."* Completing the set with 5/7 accuracy awards only "Familiar", enforcing that high levels reflect strict low-error performance.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

This is the single most critical structural mechanism for preventing badge grinding and preserving assessment integrity. By hard-gating advancement on low-error thresholds, Khan enforces that proficiency claims represent real-time competence rather than brute-force persistence. Nudging the learner to restart and consolidate immediately (`Productive Persistence`) stops them from limping to a hollow completion state.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • Careless Error Penalty & Test Anxiety: In complex multi-step analytical tasks, a minor formatting or calculation typo on Problem 6 can trigger the two-mistake gate, invalidating 15 minutes of deep conceptual work and inducing severe user frustration if minor typo forgiveness is not calibrated.
  • Memorization on Restart: If clicking "Start over" presents the exact same 7 static problems in identical order, learners simply memorize answer keys across attempts rather than mastering underlying principles.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Implement the Accuracy Gate (`FR5`) on all diagnostic skill verifications. If a learner makes >1 conceptual error, cap their award at `Familiar`. To earn `Proficient`, they must restart against a **dynamically parameterized, isomorphic problem bank**.

Required Experimental Validation

Instrument the "Start Over vs. Keep Going" decision fork upon gate triggering. Correlate whether learners who choose "Start Over" achieve higher delayed transfer accuracy than those who limp to completion.

11. Khan Academy — Productive-Struggle Remediation & Stepwise Hints

Khan Academy Stage 03: Recover & Correct Priority: P1 Adapt Now

Empirical Description (CSV Row 11)

On an incorrect submission, the platform explicitly withholds the right answer, displaying *"Not quite yet… give it another try"* alongside a "Show a step" button. Clicking reveals a progressive worked solution step-by-step (`1 / 4 — Let's substitute 14 for p`, then `+ Get another hint`), backed by an embedded 2-minute video directly inside the problem card.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

Withholding the final answer on initial failure preserves the **active retrieval attempt**, while graduated step-by-step hints break down complex multi-step derivations (`Breaking Complex Tasks into Simple Steps`). Embedding the exact 2-minute conceptual video directly within the problem card prevents tab-switching and loss of context. However, Khan's positive feedback copy (*"Nice work! You got it!"*) leans toward person-level praise—which `Hattie & Timperley` rate among the least effective feedback forms compared to process/self-regulation praise.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • Hint-Stepping to Answer Reveal: Rushed learners can click "Get another hint" 4 times in 2 seconds to exhaust the ladder and expose the final solution step (`Hint Abuse / Gaming the Scaffolding`).
  • Lack of Isomorphic Retry Post-Hint: Once all 4 hints are revealed, Khan marks the problem incorrect for mastery purposes but often does not immediately force the learner to solve a fresh, isomorphic problem to verify that the hint sequence was actually understood.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Implement the Graduated Hint Ladder (`FR4`). Whenever a learner exhausts all hints on a task, automatically generate a new, structurally identical problem (`Isomorphic Diagnostic Retry`) that must be solved without hints to confirm learning.

Required Experimental Validation

A/B test graduated step hints against immediate full solution reveal. Measure: internal problem abandonment rate and performance on the very next unassisted problem in the set.

12. Khan Academy — End-of-Set Mastery Summary & Momentum Loop

Khan Academy Stage 06: Return Priority: P1 Adapt Now

Empirical Description (CSV Row 12)

Completing a set produces a unified summary reporting the exact mastery result (`Familiar • 71%`), correct count (`5/7 correct • 475 energy pts`), an affective banner (`🔥 Awesome! You improved your skills! 🔥`), the updated unit grid, and an immediate "Up next: Lesson 3" forward routing action.

Principal Researcher's Mechanism & Psychological Teardown

This screen successfully operationalises the **Goal-Gradient Hypothesis & Momentum Continuity**. Placing the celebration immediately after real cognitive expenditure and anchoring positive affect directly to competence gain (`You improved your skills`) solidifies self-efficacy. Crucially, providing an immediate single-click "Up next" button captures behavioral inertia, pulling the learner into the next repetition before session fatigue sets in.

Critical Vulnerabilities & Construct Risks

  • The Extrinsic Reward Crowding-Out Effect: Self-determination theory (`Deci & Ryan`) warns that activity-contingent tangible rewards (like "475 energy points") can systematically crowd out intrinsic motivation if points become the primary psychological objective of the user.
  • Rushed Continuation: If "Up next" routes directly into another heavy assessment without a brief cognitive reflection beat, learners experience rapid mental fatigue, causing spike-and-crash error rates on subsequent sets.
Strategic Application for solve.education

Design our End-of-Task Summary (`FR7`) to lead 100% with verified skill gain (`Mastery Level Updated -> Verified Evidence Recorded`). Subordinate or eliminate arbitrary points to ensure the user's focus remains entirely on portfolio growth.

Required Experimental Validation

Multivariate test: compare a summary screen leading with `Mastery Level & Portfolio Evidence` vs. one leading with `Energy Points`. Measure next-session D1 return rate and long-term intrinsic persistence.

Clear Product Feature Requirements (FR1–FR8)

Plain-English feature requirements describing exactly what the platform builds and what proof it records for employers.

FR1 Activation

Goal-to-Task Router

System must accept natural-language goal inputs or structured target roles and return exactly one high-priority diagnostic verification task accompanied by explicit causal rationale and a single-click execution button.

Closing Remarks & Conclusion: The Path Forward for solve.education

Summary of our core research findings and practical next steps to build our Proof-of-Skill platform.

01 The Core Takeaway

Why Proof-of-Skill Beats Daily Streaks

Popular learning apps win by turning study into a daily game of collecting points, streaks, and badges. But when employers are deciding who to hire, arbitrary points and attendance badges aren't enough. By focusing our platform entirely on hands-on practice and verified problem-solving, `solve.education` turns daily learning activity into honest, verified evidence that candidates can actually do the job.

02 Release 1 Rule

Universal Practice Across All Domains

We do not need to restrict our launch to coding or technical programming. Whether our candidates are analyzing business spreadsheets, reviewing financial data, solving logic puzzles, or drafting strategic case communications, the same 6-step loop applies. If a learner can solve real multi-step tasks independently inside our split-screen workspace, that is the exact proof employers need.

03 Quality Protection

Strict Accuracy Keeps Badges Meaningful

As Khan Academy proved with their Two-Mistake Limit, we must never allow learners to guess or limp across the finish line just to earn a completion badge. By requiring strict accuracy for our `Proficient` status and giving instant, plain-language rule explanations when mistakes occur, every certificate earned on `solve.education` remains gold-standard and trustworthy.
