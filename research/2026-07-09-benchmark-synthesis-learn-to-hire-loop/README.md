# Research: Benchmark Synthesis For The Learn-To-Hire Loop

- **Status:** Closed
- **Started:** 2026-07-09
- **Closed:** 2026-07-09
- **Researcher:** Codex acting as Senior UI/UX Researcher

## Goal
Synthesize the existing benchmark findings and artifacts for Brilliant, DataCamp, Busuu, and Khan Academy into a decision-ready research synthesis for solve.education. The synthesis should identify which world-class learning UX patterns can strengthen solve.education's learn-to-hire loop, what evidence supports them, what risks or anti-patterns to avoid, and what should be validated before product or engineering investment. This is input to a build decision, not a new capture study.

## Scope
In scope:
- Use `raw-data/benchmark-findings.csv` as the feature index and stated objective.
- Use the existing folders under `research/` as the artifact evidence base.
- Synthesize across platforms by UX pattern and loop stage, not by competitor feature list.
- Produce decision-ready implications for Head of Product, UI/UX, and developers.
- Separate direct artifact evidence, researcher inference, and validation needs.

Out of scope:
- New platform browsing or additional product capture.
- Re-running benchmark tasks inside the competitor products.
- Making final build commitments without validation experiments.
- Financial sizing, roadmap sequencing beyond research-priority recommendations, or detailed technical architecture.
