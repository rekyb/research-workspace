# References — Certificate vs Badge Gamification for Teacher EdTech (Indonesia)

Scholarly and authoritative evidence base for this study, gathered via a deep-research
literature review (5 threads, 24 sources fetched, 25 claims adversarially verified 3-vote,
23 confirmed / 2 refuted) on 2026-07-17. Each entry: author + year, title, venue, the
1-line finding, which research question it serves, the **evidence geography** (Western/
general vs Indonesia/SEA/LMIC), and the URL. Sources are grouped by the thread they anchor.

> **Evidence-geography rule (from PLAN.md):** only 2 sources are *directly Indonesian* (PMM
> enjoyment; the 2007-08 certification evaluation) and 2 are broadly LMIC/Global South. All
> badge-mechanics, SDT, and per-element gamification evidence is **Western/general** and is
> applied to the Indonesian-teacher decision by **mechanism inference, not local replication**.
> Treat every cross-context transfer as a hypothesis to validate locally.

## Thread 1 — Certificate-of-completion effects (Q1)

- **Perdue, D. (2023).** *Why Do Learners Choose to Certify? Understanding Certification in
  MOOCs.* HEAd'23 (9th Int'l Conf. on Higher Education Advances), MIT.
  - **Finding:** Adult MOOC learners treat certification as psychologically distinct from
    completion and pursue a certificate **only when they perceive a concrete personal/
    professional use**; value is instrumental and culturally contingent (verbatim India quote:
    certificates are promotion-mandatory; Brazil separates work from personal use).
  - **Serves:** Q1 (certificate value), Q4 by analogy (credential-heavy culture).
  - **Geography:** Western/general (n=15 qualitative; India/Brazil examples analogous to
    Indonesia's civil-service credential culture, not Indonesian data).
  - URL: https://headconf.org/wp-content/uploads/pdfs/16052.pdf

- **Athey, S. & Palikot, E. (2024).** *Machine Learning to Match Job Seekers... / credential-
  sharing field experiment.* arXiv working paper 2405.00247 (Stanford / Northeastern).
  - **Finding:** RCT with 800,000+ online-certificate earners from developing countries
    lacking college degrees: a credential-sharing nudge raised new employment **+6% overall
    (+9% for cert-related jobs)**, concentrated in the **bottom employability tercile (+12%)** —
    "credentials provide the greatest value precisely where traditional signals are weakest."
  - **Serves:** Q1, Q4 (LMIC labor-market value of certificates).
  - **Geography:** LMIC (broad, developing countries). **Caveat:** measures credential
    *visibility/signaling* (LinkedIn sharing of already-earned certs), not earning-vs-none;
    arXiv working paper, peer-review status unconfirmed.
  - URL: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2405.00247

- **(Secondary) University of Michigan (2019).** *Research shows certificates boost MOOC
  completion rates.* Michigan Record (reporting on 70+ Coursera courses, 2012-16).
  - **Finding:** Paying for / pursuing a fee-based completion certificate raised student
    engagement **10-12%**. Corroborates the certificate-as-motivator direction.
  - **Serves:** Q1. **Geography:** Western/general. **Quality:** secondary (press release).
  - URL: https://record.umich.edu/articles/research-shows-certificates-boost-mooc-completion-rates/

## Thread 2 — Digital / open badge effects & cert-vs-badge (Q2, Q3)

- **Dicheva, D. et al. (2020).** *A Course Gamification approach... badges.* SIGITE'20 (via NSF
  PAR). Within-subjects, US HBCU CS course, n=14.
  - **Finding:** Badges raised voluntary practice from **7.1 → 37.3 quizzes/student**
    (Welch p=0.0055) but **did not affect intrinsic motivation** — extrinsic motivation drove
    the activity. Badges boost engagement *quantity*, not intrinsic interest.
  - **Serves:** Q2, Q3. **Geography:** Western (US), small n.
  - URL: https://par.nsf.gov/servlets/purl/10290885

- **CITE Journal (2020).** *Microcredentialing of English-Learner Teaching Skills: digital
  badges as an assessment tool.* Contemporary Issues in Technology & Teacher Education,
  20(1). Teacher candidates, n=151.
  - **Finding:** With only **1-2% grade credit, just 33-43% completed** final badging despite
    68-97% completing skill practice; candidates were "unsure about the positive impact of
    obtaining a digital badge on their future teaching career." Instructors later raised the
    incentive to 10%. **Badges fail as a completion motivator without real perceived value.**
  - **Serves:** Q2, Q3, and directly teacher-relevant. **Geography:** Western (US).
  - URL: https://citejournal.org/volume-20/issue-1-20/current-practice/microcredentialing-of-english-learner-teaching-skills-an-exploratory-study-of-digital-badges-as-an-assessment-tool/

- **Hickey, D. & Chartrand, G. (2020).** *Recognizing competencies vs. completion vs.
  participation: ideal roles for web-enabled digital badges.* Education and Information
  Technologies, 25. Analysis of 30 funded DML badge efforts.
  - **Finding:** **None** of the "competency" badges produced thriving ecosystems, while
    **4 of 5 "participation" badges did** — socially-oriented participation recognition
    sustains engagement far better than competency credentialing.
  - **Serves:** Q2, Q6 (how to frame badges). **Geography:** Western (n=5 participation, small).
  - URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10639-019-10000-w

- **(Australia) ITE badges study (2024).** *Replacing marks with digital badges in Initial
  Teacher Education.* Higher Education Research & Development, 43(?).
  - **Finding:** Replacing marks with badges had "considerable potential to improve
    engagement with feedback, motivation, and reducing grade anxiety" (but delaying marks
    itself caused anxiety). ⚠️ **One over-reach claim from this source was REFUTED** (see below).
  - **Serves:** Q2. **Geography:** Western (Australia).
  - URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/07294360.2024.2315039

- **(Finland) Brauer et al. — Competence-based assessment and digital badging in vocational
  teacher education.** ResearchGate 320864015.
  - **Finding:** Frames open badges as micro-credentials for "equal and egalitarian
    competence-based assessment" — an assessment/recognition mechanic distinct from a
    completion certificate (12 teachers earned 645 badges/year). ⚠️ **One claim tying badging
    to gamified learning-outcome gains was REFUTED** (see below).
  - **Serves:** Q2. **Geography:** Western (Finland).
  - URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/320864015

## Thread 3 — SDT / overjustification mechanism (Q3)

- **Deci, E., Koestner, R. & Ryan, R. (1999).** *A meta-analytic review of experiments
  examining the effects of extrinsic rewards on intrinsic motivation.* Psychological Bulletin,
  125(6). Canonical, 128 studies.
  - **Finding:** Completion-, engagement-, and performance-contingent tangible rewards
    **undermined free-choice intrinsic motivation** (d = -0.36, -0.40, -0.28) and dampened
    interest; **positive feedback ENHANCED** it (d = +0.33 behavior, +0.31 interest).
    Completion-contingent ≈ a certificate of completion.
  - **Serves:** Q3 (the core mechanism). **Geography:** Western/general/foundational.
    Cameron & Pierce contest the broad generalization, not these effect sizes.
  - URL: https://home.ubalt.edu/ntygmitc/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf

- **Cerasoli, C., Nicklin, J. & Ford, M. (2014).** *Intrinsic motivation and extrinsic
  incentives jointly predict performance: a 40-year meta-analysis.* Psychological Bulletin,
  140(4). k=183, N=212,468.
  - **Finding:** Intrinsic motivation better predicts **QUALITY** of performance; extrinsic
    incentives better predict **QUANTITY**. Incentives *directly* tied to performance crowd
    out intrinsic motivation more than indirectly-tied ones (rho .30 vs .45).
  - **Serves:** Q3 (the quantity/quality split that grounds the verdict). **Geography:**
    Western/general (school/work/physical domains).
  - URL: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/2014_Cerasoli_Intrinsic.pdf

## Thread 4 — Indonesia / SEA / LMIC teacher-PD context (Q4)

- **Edu Komputika Journal / UNNES (2023-24).** *PMM adoption via the Hedonic Motivation
  System Adoption Model (HMSAM).*
  - **Finding (DIRECTLY INDONESIAN):** **Hedonic motivation — curiosity and joy — significantly
    drives teachers' behavioral intention to keep using PMM (Platform Merdeka Mengajar)**, not
    utilitarian usefulness alone. Corroborated by a UTAUT2 study (Teknika, n=383 teachers,
    Malang) finding hedonic motivation significantly influences PMM intention.
  - **Serves:** Q4 (enjoyment matters for Indonesian teacher adoption). **Geography:**
    **Indonesian.** Caveat: single-institution / modest samples (n=383 replication strengthens).
  - URLs: https://journal.unnes.ac.id/journals/edukom/article/view/10741 ·
    https://ejournal.papanda.org/index.php/jirpe/article/download/2659/1865

- **Kusumawardhani, P. (2017).** *Does teacher certification program lead to better quality
  teachers? Evidence from Indonesia.* Education Economics, 25(6).
  - **Finding (DIRECTLY INDONESIAN):** The 2007-08 national certification program (professional
    allowance ≈ doubling pay) produced **no strong evidence of improved teacher quality or
    student learning**. The credential functions as a **status/pay signal, not a competence
    driver.** Corroborated by de Ree et al., "Double for Nothing?" (QJE 2018) and the World Bank.
  - **Serves:** Q4 (extrinsic-credential/signaling culture). **Geography:** **Indonesian.**
    Caveat: tied to the 2007-08 cohort, pre-dates the current PMM/PPG regime.
  - URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09645292.2017.1329405

- **(Global South) UNESCO IIOE teacher online-PD completion study (2024).** Internet and
  Higher Education. 3,529 teacher-learners, 99 countries, 7 courses (42 interviewed).
  - **Finding:** A rare LMIC/Global South evidence base on what drives teacher online-PD
    completion (individual/institutional/country factors via HLM).
  - **Serves:** Q4. **Geography:** LMIC/Global South (broad).
  - URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1096751624000253

- **(Context, secondary) RISE Programme blogs (Indonesia).** Four decades of teacher PD in
  Indonesia; career development for civil-service teachers.
  - **Finding:** Background on PD-credit and civil-service advancement incentive structure.
  - **Serves:** Q4 context. **Geography:** Indonesian. **Quality:** secondary.
  - URLs: https://riseprogramme.org/blog/four-decades-teacher-professional-development-indonesia.html ·
    https://riseprogramme.org/blog/indonesia-career-development-civil-service-teachers.html

## Threads on teacher motivation & PD completion (Q3/Q4 cross-cutting)

- **Jansen in de Wal, J. et al. (2014, relayed 2024).** Autonomous vs controlled teacher
  motivation, latent profiles, 2,360 teachers.
  - **Finding:** Teachers high in identified/intrinsic regulation engage more in PD than those
    under external pressure. **Geography:** Western (Dutch).
  - URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11618-024-01241-8

- **Anghel, E., Littenberg-Tobias, J. & von Davier, A. (2024).** Motivation and completion
  across 4 PD MOOCs, N=3,212. Education and Information Technologies.
  - **Finding (TENSION):** **Intrinsically** motivated educators were **LESS** likely to
    *complete* a PD MOOC, while **prosocially** motivated ones were **MORE** likely — even
    controlling for initial intention. "Design for intrinsic motivation" ≠ "design for
    completion." **Geography:** Western.
  - URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10639-024-12590-6

## Thread 5 — Per-mechanic evidence for other simple gamification tools (Q5)

- **ETR&D gamification-SDT meta-analysis (2024).** 35 interventions, ~2,500 participants.
  - **Finding:** Gamification's effect is **large on relatedness (g=1.776), moderate on
    autonomy (g=0.638), minimal on competence (g=0.277)** — it works mainly through
    relatedness/autonomy, not competence. **Geography:** Western/general (relatedness CI wide).
  - **Serves:** Q5 (which mechanics to prioritize).
  - URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11423-023-10337-7

- **Zeng, J. et al. (2024).** *Exploring the impact of gamification on academic performance:
  a comprehensive meta-analysis (2008-2023).* British Journal of Educational Technology, 22
  studies.
  - **Finding:** Overall pooled **g=0.782**, but **decomposed by element**: Points+Badges+
    Leaderboards **g=0.320**, Points+Leaderboards **g=0.231** (small); Levels+Badges+
    Leaderboards reportedly **negative**. The *combination* of elements matters; stacking
    competitive mechanics can go negative. **Geography:** Western/general. Caveat: exact
    element digits carried residual uncertainty (2-1 verify vote).
  - **Serves:** Q5, Q6. URL: https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/bjet.13471

- **Kivetz, R., Urminsky, O. & Zheng, Y. — The Endowed Progress Effect.** *How artificial
  advancement increases effort.* (via ResearchGate 23547282).
  - **Finding:** Giving learners artificial head-start progress toward a goal increases effort
    and completion — the empirical basis for **progress bars / goal-gradient**. **Geography:**
    Western/general.
  - **Serves:** Q5 (progress bar / goal-gradient). URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/23547282_The_Endowed_Progress_Effect_How_Artificial_Advancement_Increases_Effort

- **(Industry, non-scholarly) Duolingo — How the streak builds habit.** Duolingo blog.
  - **Finding:** Vendor account of streak mechanics for habit formation. **Flagged as vendor/
    industry, not evidence of effect** (bias watch). Useful for the *benchmark* half, not as
    proof. **Geography:** Western/general.
  - URL: https://blog.duolingo.com/how-duolingo-streak-builds-habit/

## Evidence caveats (carried into synthesis)

- **Geography:** most mechanism/badge evidence is Western; only the PMM-enjoyment and 2007-08
  certification findings are directly Indonesian. Cross-context transfer = hypothesis to test.
- **No direct head-to-head** certificate-vs-badge experiment (same course/population) was found;
  the verdict is assembled from adjacent evidence.
- **No isolated per-mechanic study** for streaks / progress bars / points / levels / checklists
  with teachers or in Indonesia; Q5 mechanics rest on the general SDT-lever + goal-gradient
  evidence and inference.
- **Source-strength:** the strongest LMIC certificate result is an arXiv working paper measuring
  signaling (not earning); several primary studies are small (n=14, n=15) or single-institution.
- **Time-sensitivity:** Indonesian certification evidence is the 2007-08 cohort, pre-PMM/PPG.

## Peer-review context sources (added 2026-07-17, verified during `/review-research`)

Retrieved by the Domain Expert during the peer-review debate and independently re-verified
before logging. These ground the *context premises* (F6 connectivity, F8 culture, F1 PPG
hook); they do **not** remove the core caveat that mechanic *effects* remain Western-by-inference.

- **Hofstede — Indonesia country cultural profile.** Individualism **IDV = 14** (one of the
  world's lowest; world average ≈ 43), Power Distance ≈ 78. A strongly collectivist,
  high-power-distance, harmony-preferring culture.
  - **Serves:** F8 (reword "collectivist" precisely), and the F1↔F8 reconciliation (the same
    culture that avoids public individual competition strongly rewards status credentials).
  - **Geography:** Indonesian. **Quality:** reputable secondary (Hofstede index aggregator);
    IDV=14 is the canonical Hofstede value, corroborated across multiple sources.
  - URL: https://internationalbusinesscenter.org/geert-hofstede/hofstede_indonesia.shtml

- **DataReportal — Digital 2025: Indonesia.** Internet penetration 74.6% (~212M users);
  **356M active cellular mobile connections ≈ 125% of population**, i.e. a strongly
  mobile-first population.
  - **Serves:** F6 (mobile-first / offline-first design requirement).
  - **Geography:** Indonesian. **Quality:** industry-authoritative aggregator (We Are Social /
    Meltwater / DataReportal).
  - URL: https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2025-indonesia

- **UNICEF — Situation Analysis on Digital Learning in Indonesia.** Documents a substantial
  **digital divide**: unequal internet access and poor bandwidth by income and geography, with
  rural/remote and lower-income households (and girls/women) least able to engage in online
  learning.
  - **Serves:** F6 (connectivity-constrained teacher premise; supports forgiving streaks +
    offline-first).
  - **Geography:** Indonesian. **Quality:** authoritative (UNICEF). **Caveat:** the precise
    "~21% vs ~93%" access figures cited in the debate were **not** independently verified here;
    only the *qualitative* income/geography divide is logged as confirmed.
  - URL: https://www.unicef.org/indonesia/reports/situation-analysis-digital-learning-indonesia

- **PPG (Pendidikan Profesi Guru) / Sertifikat Pendidik — current Indonesian teacher-
  certification regime.** The *Sertifikat Pendidik* is the official educator credential issued
  via PPG; certified teachers receive a professional allowance (TPG) tied to pay, and the
  credential gates civil-service career progression. Corroborated by the *"Is Double Salary
  Enough? Examining Civil Servant Teachers' Welfare as the Effect of Teacher Certification
  Policy in Indonesia"* analysis.
  - **Serves:** F1 (modernizes the incentive anchor off the 2007-08 cohort; the PPG/PD-credit
    hook the product can *feed, not issue*).
  - **Geography:** Indonesian. **Quality:** government regime + academic analysis. **Caveat:**
    the specific SMERU citation named in the debate was not located; the claim rests on the
    official PPG regime and the corroborating certification-welfare paper.
  - URLs: https://ppg.kemendikdasmen.go.id/ ·
    https://www.researchgate.net/publication/396335162

- **MGMP (Musyawarah Guru Mata Pelajaran) — subject-teacher working groups.** Indonesia's
  established collaborative/peer teacher-PD structure. Logged as **context** for the prosocial/
  collective lever; the lever's *effect* evidence rests on Anghel et al. 2024 (prosocially-
  motivated educators complete more) plus the low-individualism profile above, not on a specific
  MGMP study (none independently verified here).
  - **Serves:** prosocial/collective framing lever. **Geography:** Indonesian. **Quality:**
    established institution / context, not a cited effect study.
