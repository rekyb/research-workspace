# Coursera: certificate presentation flow

**Summary (entry → goal):** A logged-out learner lands on a course page and tries to
understand what they get for finishing. Coursera surfaces the completion certificate as a
**shareable, LinkedIn-ready credential**, then gates enrolment behind account creation (and,
for the certificate itself, payment). Captured on the free course *Learning How to Learn*.

1. **Learner opens the course page.** The hero shows the course title, provider (Deep
   Teaching Solutions), instructors, and a prominent **"Enroll now"** button. Nothing about
   the reward yet, the pitch is the learning.
   *Evidence: `screenshots/01-course-hero.png`, start of `flow.gif`.*

2. **Learner scrolls to "Details to know."** Coursera presents three cards. The first is
   **"Shareable certificate"** with the sub-label **"Add to your LinkedIn profile"** and a
   LinkedIn icon. The certificate is framed not as proof-of-learning but as a **credential you
   display** to signal to employers.
   *Evidence: `screenshots/02-shareable-certificate.png`.*

3. **Learner clicks "Enroll now" to see what it costs.** Instead of the course, a modal
   appears: **"Log in or create account."** Enrolment (and therefore any progress toward the
   certificate) is gated behind account creation; email, Google, Facebook, or Apple.
   *Evidence: `screenshots/03-auth-gate.png`, end of `flow.gif`.*
   **Friction / dead-end for this study:** the certificate itself sits behind this auth wall
   and, beyond it, a paywall. Per workspace guardrails I did not create an account or pay, so
   the post-purchase certificate artifact was not captured. The observable free surface stops
   at the auth gate.

**Where the motivational mechanic lives:** step 2. The certificate's pull is its *external
signalling value* ("add to your LinkedIn profile"), captured *before* the learner has done
any work, exactly the pre-completion framing the plan asked us to look for.
