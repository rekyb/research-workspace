# Duolingo: observations & patterns

**Role in this study:** the **gamification-heavy** reference; how *simple gamification tools*
(Q5) are presented when they are the core loop, not an add-on. Captured in an **anonymous
guest session**, so no PII (account holder or third party) appears, no login used.

## What stood out (the simple-mechanics inventory, all in 2 screens)
- **Progress bar / goal-gradient**: the Daily Quest "Earn 10 XP" shows a 0/10 bar
  (`02-…`, `03-…`). Direct instance of the endowed-progress / goal-gradient mechanic
  (Kivetz et al.), the highest-fit simple tool for our audience.
- **Points / XP**: XP is the universal currency of progress and the unit quests are measured in.
- **Streak**: a flame counter in the HUD (habit lever).
- **Levels / path**: a leveled lesson map with locked nodes, checkpoints, and a treasure
  chest (unlockables).
- **Leaderboards, locked/teased**: "Unlock Leaderboards! Complete 3 more lessons to start
  competing." Competition is **earned**, not imposed; the pre-reward state is used as a hook.
  Note the literature caution that competitive stacks show small-to-negative effects, Duolingo
  mitigates by delaying/optional-ising it.
- **Quests + time-boxing**: daily quests that "refresh every day" with a countdown ("17
  HOURS"), plus "More quests unlock soon" (progressive reveal).
- **Badges tied to challenges**: "Monthly challenges… earn exclusive badges." Badges here are
  **participation/achievement recognition inside the loop**, not a terminal credential. This
  matches the literature's best role for badges (Hickey & Chartrand 2020, participation > competency).

## Patterns worth synthesizing
- **Stack simple mechanics into a continuous loop** rather than relying on one terminal reward.
  Progress bar + XP + streak + quests keep motivation between sessions.
- **Tease rewards before they are earned** (locked leaderboard, "unlock soon" quests) to create
  a pre-completion pull, the same principle as showing a certificate before completion.
- **Make competition opt-in / earned.** Consistent with evidence that leaderboards can backfire;
  Duolingo unlocks them only after initial success.
- **Badges as in-loop recognition, not the finish line.** Exactly the split the synthesis argues
  for: certificate = terminal credential, badge = participation/progress recognition.

## Gaps / caveats
- Guest session shows the *new-user* state (streak 0, leaderboard locked). The mature-account
  states (active streak, league standings, earned achievement badges) were not captured because
  that requires an account (guardrails; also avoids third-party leaderboard PII).
