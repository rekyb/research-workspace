# Google for Education (Teacher Center): observations & patterns

**Role in this study:** the **teacher-PD counterpart** to Coursera; how a credential aimed
specifically at educators is framed. Captured logged-out.

## What stood out
- **Same "resume credential" framing as Coursera,** stated outright: "Certification looks
  good on you, and your resume" (`01-certification-hero.png`). Reinforces that certificates
  are sold on external signalling value, and here the audience *is* teachers.
- **Certificate turned into levels.** Two tiers, **Level 1 → Level 2**
  (`02-tiered-free-credentials.png`), give the credential a **progression** structure. This is
  the certificate mechanic borrowing a simple gamification tool (levels): a reason to come
  back and climb, which a one-shot certificate does not provide.
- **Free of charge.** Both levels are "Valid for 3 years | Free of charge." Removing the
  paywall matters for a low-and-middle-income teacher audience; the credential's value is the
  signal, not scarcity. Contrast with Coursera's paid model.
- **Structured "Get certified" path.** Each level has a "Prepare by…" checklist (exam guide,
  fundamentals course), a lightweight checklist/onboarding mechanic toward the credential.
- **Localises to Indonesia.** Google detected the Indonesia region and offered a "Teacher"
  role filter (dismissed, no data submitted), evidence the credential flow is presented to
  exactly this study's audience.

## Patterns worth synthesizing
- **Tiered credentials (levels) beat a single certificate for repeat engagement.** Level 1 →
  Level 2 gives progression and a next goal.
- **Free + credential-signal** is a viable model when the audience values the signal (teachers,
  LMIC) and you are not monetising the certificate itself.
- **"Valid for 3 years"** adds mild scarcity/renewal, keeping the credential live.

## Gaps / caveats
- The post-exam issued badge/certificate artifact is behind the exam (and account); not
  captured. Evidence is the public framing and the tier structure.
