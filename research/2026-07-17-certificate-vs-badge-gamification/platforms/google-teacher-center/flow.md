# Google for Education: teacher certification flow

**Summary (entry → goal):** A teacher explores how to get certified in Google tools. Google
frames certification as a **resume credential** and offers **two free, tiered levels**
(Certified Educator Level 1 → Level 2), turning a certificate into a lightweight
**progression / levels** mechanic. Captured logged-out on the Google for Education
Certifications page.

1. **Teacher lands on the Certifications page.** The hero reads **"Certification looks good
   on you, and your resume,"** beside a **"Google Certified Educator — Level 2"** badge-style
   card. The pitch, like Coursera, is external signalling (your resume), and the credential is
   shown as a visual badge before any work.
   *Evidence: `screenshots/01-certification-hero.png`, start of `flow.gif`.*

2. **Teacher scrolls to the certification options.** Two side-by-side cards present a tiered
   path: **Level 1** ("Demonstrate your mastery of Google tools in your classroom") and
   **Level 2** ("Validate your expertise and advanced technology integration skills"). Both
   read **"Valid for 3 years | Free of charge."** Each card has a **"Get certified → Prepare
   by…"** checklist (exam guide, fundamentals training).
   *Evidence: `screenshots/02-tiered-free-credentials.png`.*
   **Delight:** the credential is **free** and **leveled**, so a teacher can start at Level 1
   and climb, a progression hook a single certificate lacks.

3. **(Incidental) Region + role detection.** While scrolling, Google surfaced a region nudge
   ("You're now viewing content for a different region: **Indonesia**") and a role chooser
   listing **"Teacher, Instructor or Professor."** I submitted no data and dismissed both.
   Noted only because it shows the flow is localisable to the Indonesian teacher audience.

**Where the motivational mechanic lives:** step 2. Unlike Coursera's single paid certificate,
Google turns the credential into **tiers/levels** (Level 1 → Level 2), a progression mechanic,
while keeping the same "resume credential" signalling and removing the paywall.
